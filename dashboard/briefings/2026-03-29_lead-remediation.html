Executive Summary

Lead remediation in Syracuse is progressing under federal EPA mandate and state action-level requirements, with the city demonstrating sustained compliance and proactive replacement strategy. The Department of Public Works, led by Commissioner (name not specified in records), has secured $2M in one-time federal grant funding for lead service line inventory and $5M in bonded debt from 2025 to fund replacements beyond core infrastructure projects. The city is currently executing Phase 1 of a 2,900-home private-side lead service line replacement program, targeting properties where public-side work was completed in 2005-2007. An EPA mandate effective November 2027 requires full service line replacement (main to meter); Syracuse is already exceeding this minimum by completing full replacements proactively.
