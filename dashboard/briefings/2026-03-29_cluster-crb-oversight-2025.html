Executive Summary

Syracuse's Citizen Review Board (CRB) has experienced acute governance instability over the past 12 months, marked by leadership turnover, operational backlog, and unresolved structural tensions between the Common Council and the board itself. After chair Bryn Lovejoy-Grinnell's abrupt resignation in April 2025 citing lack of independence and city support (CTX-NEWS-2025-00272), the board stabilized operationally under new administrator Nolita Tanyhill (hired May 2025) and chair Don Johnson, who have cleared complaint backlogs and worked through cases exceeding the 18-month statute of limitations (CTX-NEWS-2025-00159). However, political dysfunction persists: Council member Chol Majok's April 2025 proposal to establish a Citizen Review Board Legislative Update Committee—intended to modernize the police watchdog law unchanged since 2011—was withdrawn following pushback from council colleagues and the former CRB chair about timing (CTX-NEWS-2025-00182). Most recently, in January 2026, the Public Safety Committee chair signaled receptiveness to legislative reforms that could expand the CRB (CTX-NEWS-2025-00159), while simultaneously, council scrutiny flagged unfilled CRB appointments from both the council and mayor beyond the legislative deadline (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00011). The board remains operationally functional but politically trapped between council autonomy claims and independence requirements.
