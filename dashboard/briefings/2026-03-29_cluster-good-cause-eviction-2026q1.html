Executive Summary

The Good Cause Eviction (GCE) local law was defeated on February 23, 2026, in a 4-4 tie vote at the Syracuse Common Council, ending four years of deliberation on the measure. The proposal, which would have applied protections to landlords owning two or more properties, split the council along lines that reflect deep divisions on housing policy and tenant protections. Four councilors voted in favor (Caldwell, Majok, Ehrenreich, Williams), while four opposed (Nave, Moore, Jones-Rowser, Monto). Council Member Moore cited unanswered questions about code enforcement as justification for the defeat. The loss marks a significant setback for housing equity advocates and represents a critical moment in Syracuse's ongoing housing crisis, coinciding with parallel development initiatives (Parkside Commons) and broader fiscal pressures on the city's housing and social services portfolios.

The GCE debate occurred in a broader context of council scrutiny over housing and equity issues. The council simultaneously advanced the Parkside Commons redevelopment (a 393-unit mixed-income project requiring tax exemptions and shelter rent agreements), held multiple lead remediation and water safety hearings, and wrestled with procurement processes for equity-focused initiatives. The defeat of GCE suggests the council's appetite for tenant protections is limited—despite progressive voices on equity and community engagement elsewhere in the agenda—and may reflect concerns about implementation costs, code enforcement capacity, and landlord compliance burdens.
