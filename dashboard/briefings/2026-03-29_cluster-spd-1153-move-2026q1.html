Executive Summary

The Syracuse Police Department and Fire Department are executing a major facilities relocation from the Public Safety Building (PSB) to 1153 West Fayette Street, with total capital investment of approximately $1.5M in bond funding plus ancillary operational costs. First Deputy Chief Rich Trudeau is leading the initiative, which includes IT infrastructure upgrades ($200K), audiovisual modernization ($497K), officer wellness facilities (functional fitness gym), and parking design across three connected sites. The project has advanced through City Council with generally positive sentiment, though significant scrutiny remains around IT procurement processes, parking lot design (pump track relocation), and audiovisual equipment contract award procedures. Key milestones achieved: $1M bond authorization passed (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00130), revised Schedule A cleared by bond counsel (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00078), and AV/FFE contract amendments executed (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00091). Principal remaining friction point: community opposition to elimination of an existing pump track recreational facility, with Councilor Caldwell advocating for alternative parking site at 1320 West Fayette (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00095, CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00136).
