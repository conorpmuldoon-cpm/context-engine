Friction Points

Aquarium vs. Concrete Plant — McMahon publicly opposes Heidelberg Materials' proposed concrete plant 2 blocks from the county's $100M aquarium at Inner Harbor. The city holds permitting authority; the county has only political influence. McMahon offered to help relocate the facility. This illustrates the core jurisdictional tension: county invests heavily in areas where the city controls land use.

Homeless Services Siting — City and county paused a daytime homeless drop-in center on W. Onondaga Street after neighborhood opposition. The city effectively vetoed a county-led facility siting (funded by opioid settlement money), revealing coordination friction on sensitive social services.

Housing Pipeline Gap — McMahon claims a 17,000+ unit housing pipeline, but investigative journalism found only ~8,000 units sufficiently defined. The county needs 1,700 units/year through 2040 for Micron-driven growth; 2024 production was only 588. Housing permits are at a 25-year low.

AIM Formula Reform

Aid and Incentives for Municipalities (AIM) is Syracuse's 2nd-largest revenue source, frozen since 2012. State Senator Christopher Ryan and City Auditor Alexander Marion published a reform letter (March 2025) advocating for a commission to redesign the formula. Hochul provided a modest $50M statewide infusion ($5M to Syracuse), but the underlying formula remains unchanged. Less than half of Syracuse properties pay property taxes, compounding the reliance on state aid.

I-81 Community Grid ($2.25B)

NYSDOT's viaduct replacement is the largest state project in Syracuse — a $2.25B transformation due for completion in 2028. The Community Grid design replaces the elevated highway with tree-lined medians, pedestrian crosswalks, and bike lanes. Driving housing development along the corridor (600+ apartments planned). Communication gaps between NYSDOT and city flagged (a noise monitor near City Hall was misidentified as a suspicious device).
