Executive Summary

The Department of Assessment is a small but strategically active department managing property valuations, land dispositions, lease agreements, and tax-revenue-related decisions. Led by Commissioner Matt OJ with Deputy Commissioner Michael Layman handling most council presentations, the department has been heavily engaged in three major initiatives: (1) the Parkside Commons mixed-income housing redevelopment shelter rent agreement; (2) property appraisals and right-of-way valuations tied to capital planning; and (3) technical corrections and systems migration. The department operates at the intersection of tax revenue (Assessment corrections directly impact city revenue projections) and capital planning (property sales, lease authorizations, and appraisals). Current fiscal context shows Assessment contributing approximately $1M in budget savings in FY2026 because the planned property revaluation has not occurred (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00148). The department has maintained a procedural, low-controversy posture in recent months, with council scrutiny focused primarily on the Parkside Commons shelter rent structure rather than core assessment operations.

Recent Council Activity

Parkside Commons Shelter Rent (Primary Focus) The Parkside Commons Phase 1 redevelopment has generated the most council attention. On 2026-01-22, the Department of Assessment (via Deputy Commissioner Layman) requested a 15-year shelter rent agreement from Common Council for the BFC Partners/SAEVI joint venture project (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00041). The council expressed deep skepticism, with members directly contradicting developer claims about maintenance response times based on constituent complaints. Layman characterized the shelter rent as "consistent with and at ceiling of standard practice," but Councilor Williams challenged the shelter rent figures and questioned how the city would realize future revenue (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00092). The item was held multiple times (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00090, CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00142) with Assessment Commissioner OJ and Councilor Williams meeting offline on 2026-03-04 to negotiate before a potential Monday vote. As of 2026-03-18, the item remained held (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00090).
