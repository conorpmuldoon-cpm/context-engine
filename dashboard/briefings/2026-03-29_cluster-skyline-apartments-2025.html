Executive Summary

The Skyline Apartments cluster represents a high-stakes enforcement intervention by Mayor Ben Walsh's administration against a private developer for breach of renovation commitments. In April 2025, the City filed suit against Chicago-based Clear Investment Group, seeking court appointment of a receiver and $2M in escrow for repairs after the company acquired the 352-unit building (rebranded as "The Metropolitan") in 2023 with promises of a $4.6M rehabilitation plan but failed to execute (CTX-NEWS-2025-00338, CTX-NEWS-2025-00004).

By late September 2025, a legal settlement was reached in which Clear Investment committed to completing renovations and making 350+ units occupancy-ready within seven months (by April 30, 2026), with weekly city inspections and phased certification requirements (CTX-NEWS-2025-00330). As of October 2025, the company reported progress: modernized elevators, upgraded fire and security systems, and completion of the first three floors, targeting partial occupancy by December 31, 2025 (CTX-NEWS-2025-00141). The settlement reflects the Mayor's explicit prioritization of housing quality and resident safety while imposing accountability mechanisms on private developers.

Clear Investment Group is simultaneously renovating a second major complex, The Summit (formerly Vincent Apartments, 260 units), acquired in the same 2023 transaction. Both projects encountered $5.3M in unexpected costs including a major flood (CTX-NEWS-2025-00304). The cluster signals heightened city enforcement posture on housing code compliance and developer accountability—a strategic pivot from welcoming private investment to litigating performance.
