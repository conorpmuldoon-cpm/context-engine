DPW to Begin Overnight Leaf and Snow Removal Operations

Published on November 01, 2025

The transition from fall to winter in the City of Syracuse is approaching. Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh announced the Department of Public Works (DPW) will launch its overnight shift for the late fall and winter months beginning on Sunday night, November 2. Crews who are on duty to plow streets in the event of early morning snowfall will be deployed to pick up leaves on city streets when it's not snowing.

“As temperatures drop and the weather patterns shift, it’s important that we stay ready for whatever conditions Syracuse throws our way,” said Mayor Ben Walsh. “Our DPW crews work tirelessly to make sure roads are safe and neighborhoods stay clear. The overnight shift allows us to respond quickly to early snow while keeping up with the leaf collection across the city.”

The city provides leaf pickup as a service to residents in November with work taking place during both daytime and overnight hours. Leaf pickup vehicles are equipped with audible beeps for safety, and some units create scraping and banging noises while collecting loose leaves and branches.

“We know the noise from overnight collection can be disruptive, and we truly appreciate residents’ patience,” said Jeremy Robinson, Commissioner of Public Works. “Our goal is to clear leaves efficiently before they cause flooding or slow down snowplow operations. Bagging leaves helps our crews work faster and quieter, keeping the process safe and effective for everyone.”

Residents are urged to place all leaves in paper bags and never on the roadway. Leaves should be placed between the sidewalk and curb, separated from all other waste. They must not block fire hydrants, poles, trees, vents, or catch basins. 

“When leaves are placed in the streets, they can block storm drains and cause localized flooding,” said Commissioner Robinson. “They also clog our underground sewer systems, which can lead to backups and property damage. Keeping leaves off the roadway protects both residents and city infrastructure.”

Residents may also use the City’s yard waste drop-off site at 1200 Canal Street Extension, accessible from South Midler Avenue between Interstate 690 and Erie Boulevard. The site accepts brush, branches, and leaves and operates from 7 a.m. to 3 p.m., Monday through Friday.

For more information about leaf collection and winter preparations, visit syr.gov/winter.
