## Executive Summary

Syracuse launched a deer management pilot program in 2025 that has become a multi-benefit urban wildlife initiative addressing three city priorities simultaneously: public safety (vehicle-deer collision reduction), public health (Lyme disease tick prevention), and food security (venison donation to the Food Bank of Central New York). The program culled 137 white-tailed deer between January and March 2025, generating 16,500 pounds of venison that translated to approximately 60,000 meals for the Food Bank to date. The initiative uses a combination of traps and USDA wildlife officers in high-density residential neighborhoods where firearm use is unsafe. 

Chief Policy Officer Greg Loh has positioned the program as a response to urban deer overpopulation, but the initiative faces constituent resistance from residents who value the deer presence in their neighborhoods—a dynamic that creates political complexity even as the program delivers measurable public health and food security outcomes. The administration expects the program to continue, though expansion timing and scope remain under review pending community feedback.
