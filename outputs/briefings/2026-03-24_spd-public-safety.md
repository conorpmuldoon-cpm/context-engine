## 3. Axon License Plate Readers (CLUSTER-AXON-LPR-2026Q1)

**6 records | Jan 21 – Feb 9, 2026 | Council segments**

Council conducted extensive deliberation before unanimously approving a 26-camera Axon LPR contract (expanding from 13 cameras).

**Key findings from joint committee review (Feb 6):**
- SPD is **sole data owner** in a closed system — no vendor access
- **No ICE data sharing** absent court order/subpoena
- **Opt-in by default** (unlike Flock, which accidentally left sharing enabled)
- **30-day automatic deletion** for unused data; evidence follows crime-specific retention
- Cameras placed at traffic convergence points across all four city quadrants
- Vehicles involved in ~70% of crimes per SPD data
- Real-life example cited: north side homicide solved via LPR

**Council champion:** Jimmy Monto publicly defended the contract as "highly and easily revocable." Public attendees disrupted the February 9 vote but the contract passed unanimously.

**Open item:** Flock camera contract was "held" (not cancelled) to prevent service lapse during Axon transition. No record confirms whether Flock was officially terminated.

## 4. PBA / Armory Square Dispute (CLUSTER-PBA-ARMORY-SQUARE-2025Q3)

**8 records | Jul 14–27, 2025 | All news**

A two-week public crisis triggered by PBA President Joe Moran's Facebook post describing Armory Square as "chaotic" with "gangbangers" and claiming 40 arrests in a single night (most were for open containers/loitering).

**Escalation timeline:**
1. **Jul 14** — PBA post published. Mayor Walsh, Chief Cecile, and Deputy Mayor Owens immediately criticized.
2. **Jul 15** — Moran doubled down. NAACP Regional Director Linda Brown-Robinson called language "racially coded."
3. **Jul 16** — Moran posted that officers should disregard de-escalation policies. Cecile clarified PBA President has no authority to direct operational policy.
4. **Jul 21** — Cecile's official Armory Square safety statement credited coordinated strategy.
5. **Jul 23** — Cecile publicly called Moran's comments "dangerous and reckless."
6. **Jul 27** — Moran partially walked back statements. Syracuse.com editorial board condemned the posts.

**Political significance:** This was a rare public rupture between police leadership and the union, with the Chief, Mayor, Deputy Mayor, NAACP, and editorial board all aligned against the PBA President. No follow-up records show whether formal resolution occurred or social media governance policies were adopted.
