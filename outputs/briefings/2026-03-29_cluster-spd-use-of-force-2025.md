## Executive Summary

The Syracuse Police Department faces sustained public and judicial scrutiny over use-of-force incidents spanning from a fatal 2016 shooting through April 2025 viral arrests. Two distinct but overlapping crises define this cluster: (1) a high-profile civil litigation against Officer Kelsey Francemone for the 2016 fatal shooting of Gary Porter, and (2) public backlash against SPD "tactical strikes" documented in a viral April 9, 2025 arrest video on South Avenue.

However, the Porter litigation reveals deeper institutional vulnerabilities: a federal jury found excessive force but qualified immunity doctrine is expected to overturn the verdict despite a $10,000 judgment. The Common Council's May 2025 budget cuts (which targeted $3.5M from police funding before mayoral veto restoration) signal legislative skepticism about departmental accountability, with Councilor Pat Hogan framing settlement rejection as a moral position tied to broader civil rights concerns (CTX-NEWS-2025-00302).

The cluster sits at the intersection of fiscal constraint, federal litigation risk, and constituent pressure.
