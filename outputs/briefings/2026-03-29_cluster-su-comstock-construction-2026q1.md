## Executive Summary

Syracuse University's three-building sophomore housing initiative on Comstock Avenue represents a $360M construction commitment ($253M hard cost for the two Comstock buildings alone) that will employ 600–700 workers and house 1,299 additional beds across Waverly (762 beds) and Comstock (537 beds) facilities. The project hinges on an unprecedented **2-year full closure of Comstock Avenue**, triggering a fee structure crisis: the city's 2025 road closure fee schedule, which compounds every 14 days, would generate approximately **$2.6M in fees** — a scenario the fee schedule's architects never contemplated. 

The Public Works Committee, chaired by Dr. Chol Majok, convened on March 17, 2026 (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00114, CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00117) to review the project with Hayner Hoyt Corporation president Jeremy Thurston and SU Director of Campus Planning Joel Fury. The central tension: the city administration has requested fee schedule modification to reduce the two-year closure fee burden, but Council members are closely scrutinizing the request, raising precedent-setting concerns and invoking the city's structural budget deficit. The chair has signaled support for development balanced with fiscal accountability, but the resolution remains unresolved as of March 29, 2026.
