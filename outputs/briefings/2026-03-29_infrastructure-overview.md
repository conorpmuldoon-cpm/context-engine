## Executive Summary

Syracuse is in the midst of a multi-year infrastructure modernization effort spanning IT systems, public safety, transportation, water remediation, and downtown revitalization. The city has committed approximately $7.8M over five years (2020–2025) to payroll and human capital modernization, with $3.7M spent and ~$2.5M remaining in approved funds (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00132). Simultaneously, Mayor Sharon Owens and the Common Council have secured $30M in additional state aid from the New York State Senate One-House Budget proposal to support infrastructure, housing, and economic development initiatives (CTX-WEB-2026-00018, CTX-WEB-2026-00027), demonstrating strong intergovernmental alignment on capital investment.

However, council scrutiny is intensifying. Finance Committee Chair Corey Williams and Councilor Ehrenreich have publicly questioned the trajectory of payroll modernization projects, audit findings reveal repeat material weaknesses in capital project tracking and grant reimbursement (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00149), and the closure of the Fayette Street Parking Garage due to structural deterioration signals broader deferred maintenance concerns (CTX-NEWS-2025-00333). The city's infrastructure strategy reflects ambitious goals—Vision Zero traffic safety, lead service line remediation, pedestrian/bicycle connectivity, broadband equity, and public safety capital improvements—but execution faces resource constraints, vendor management challenges, and the need for stronger asset lifecycle planning.

## Recent Council Activity

**Payroll Modernization & IT Systems (Ongoing scrutiny)**

The Finance Committee convened multiple sessions in February 2026 to review payroll and human capital modernization. CFO Josh Syrson, HR Assistant Director Mike Smith, and analytics staff presented a comprehensive project history: SWC (Staffing Workforce Control) digital timekeeping is at 70% live across city departments (TeleStaff for public safety, SWC for civilian departments) (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00119). Water Department is fully live; Parks near-complete; additional rollout phased to ~250 employees per month to manage support bandwidth (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00119).

Committee members demanded cost justification and questioned vendor selection. Finance Chair Ehrenreich explicitly asked why standard payroll processors (e.g., Paychex) weren't adopted, and Council noted the project has accumulated $7.8M in spend across SWC and Oracle platforms since 2020 (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00123). Costs ballooned in 2024 due to EY consulting. Terminated contractors include EY, ERP1, Dear Alien Bait, and Cherro Technologies; active contracts remain with UKG (Kronos license) and Kathleen LLC (staffing) (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00123).

**Key unresolved issues:** SWC end-of-life scheduled March 2027; parallel rollout of UKG Pro HCM system underway via Mosaic Consulting Group agreement (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00132). Training plan includes employee-facing website with video courses, manager-specific modules, and same-day help desk response for live departments (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00124). Council expressed hesitancy about additional major expenditures and demanded benchmarking against peer city models (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00124). No new funding requested—$3.7M of $6.25M approved budget remains.

**Water & Lead Remediation (Compliance & Proactive Replacement)**
