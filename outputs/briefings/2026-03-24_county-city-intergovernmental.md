### Friction Points

**Aquarium vs. Concrete Plant** — McMahon publicly opposes Heidelberg Materials' proposed concrete plant 2 blocks from the county's $100M aquarium at Inner Harbor. The city holds permitting authority; the county has only political influence. McMahon offered to help relocate the facility. **Homeless Services Siting** — City and county paused a daytime homeless drop-in center on W. Onondaga Street after neighborhood opposition. The city effectively vetoed a county-led facility siting (funded by opioid settlement money), revealing coordination friction on sensitive social services.

**Housing Pipeline Gap** — McMahon claims a 17,000+ unit housing pipeline, but investigative journalism found only ~8,000 units sufficiently defined. The county needs 1,700 units/year through 2040 for Micron-driven growth; 2024 production was only 588. Housing permits are at a 25-year low.
