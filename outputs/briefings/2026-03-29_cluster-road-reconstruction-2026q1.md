## Executive Summary

Syracuse's road reconstruction program represents the city's sustained multi-year commitment to infrastructure maintenance and modernization. Mayor Ben Walsh has anchored this initiative through consistent public announcements (CTX-WEB-2026-00082, CTX-WEB-2026-00087, CTX-WEB-2026-00094), highlighting 2024 plans encompassing nearly 23 miles of repaving and 30 miles of seal-and-resurface work across all quadrants, funded through local, state, and federal sources. The program demonstrates fiscal discipline—DPW and Department of Engineering have executed projects using data-driven prioritization and are managing scope creep (e.g., Midland Avenue experienced a $620K overrun due to NYS DOT delays, covered by local funds; CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00094).

As of Q1 2026, the road program is active but increasingly intersecting with competing capital demands. The City Council is closely scrutinizing budget performance: the TIP signal interconnect phase 2 (North-South/East-West corridors) overran by $260K to ~$4.6M (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00083), and the Lodi Street project experienced unspecified overruns (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00061). Simultaneously, pedestrian safety infrastructure has emerged as a parallel political priority, with substantial council advocacy for sidewalk and accessibility improvements tied to Vision Zero (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00048, CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00050, CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00051). A $3.5M federally and state-funded Regional Market area pedestrian and bicycle project is in preliminary design (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00048).

The broader context includes an unprecedented 2-year full closure of Comstock Avenue for Syracuse University residential construction, generating administrative friction over road closure fees ($2.6M estimated; CTX-MANUAL-2026-00003). This has surfaced questions about fee schedule sustainability and precedent-setting for major institutional projects.

## Budget Context

**Capital Planning and TIP Funding**

Road reconstruction is primarily funded through Transportation Improvement Program (TIP) allocations, CHIPS (Consolidated Highway Improvement Program) reimbursement, and local match funds. As of Q1 2026, TIP allocation appears constrained:

- **Signal Interconnect Phase 2 (North-South/East-West):** $4.6M total cost with 32% local share ($1.472M), as no TIP funding is available (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00083).
- **Midland Avenue Paving:** $5.286M final cost; overrun absorbed by local funds (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00094).
- **James Street TIP (South Salina to Grant Blvd):** CNS Engineers received a $29K increase for engineering; construction estimated ~2028 (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00062).

**CHIPS Reimbursement Success**

Salt barn replacement ($4M, 100% CHIPS-reimbursable, October 15 completion target) demonstrates strong state partnership for facility infrastructure (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00061). Council praised CHIPS reimbursement process reforms as streamlining cost recovery (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00125).

**Road Closure Fee Revenue**

SU Comstock Avenue 2-year closure is estimated to generate $2.6M in road closure fees under the current fee schedule (adopted 2025). This represents approximately $600,000 per 90-day period based on ~30,000 sq ft curb-to-curb at $500–200 per sq ft in 14-day renewal periods (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00117, CTX-MANUAL-2026-00004). The administration has not disclosed whether road closure fees are dedicated to road maintenance or general fund.

**Vision Zero and Pedestrian Infrastructure Funding**

A $3.5M federally and state-funded Regional Market area pedestrian and bicycle infrastructure project is in preliminary design (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00048). NYS DOT Transportation Alternatives grant is being pursued for Westside pedestrian and bicycle safety improvements (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00082).
