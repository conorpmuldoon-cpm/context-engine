## Executive Summary

The EV Charging Pilot represents a $400K+ infrastructure initiative anchored at Progress Park, combining NYS DEC and National Grid grant funding to deploy two Level 3 fast chargers. The project has advanced from heated procurement debate to council approval (February 2026), but remains politically sensitive due to recurring equity concerns about competitive bidding waivers and local small-business access to emerging clean-energy sectors.

The primary vendor, JCOM (Canadaigua-based, 40+ municipal clients), secured a controversial competitive bid waiver to handle grant writing, design, and turnkey deployment. This waiver triggered sustained council pushback led by Councilor Caldwell, who characterized it as symptomatic of the city's pattern of excluding minority-owned and union-affiliated businesses. Despite these objections, the council ultimately approved the JCOM agreement in late February 2026 after securing commitments for MBE/WBE local participation and clarifying site selection (Progress Park chosen due to recent National Grid substation upgrade enabling faster service).

The pilot sits within a broader climate-leadership agenda championed by Mayor Owens, who simultaneously launched Syracuse Community Solar (March 2026) with affordability and equity guardrails. The EV charging project signals municipal commitment to transportation electrification but has exposed governance friction around how the city balances grant deadline pressure against competitive procurement norms.
