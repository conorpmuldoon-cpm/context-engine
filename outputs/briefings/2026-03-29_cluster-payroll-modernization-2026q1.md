## Executive Summary

The City of Syracuse is in the final approval and implementation phase of a payroll modernization project that has consumed $7.8M over five years (2020–2025) and faces significant credibility challenges with the Common Council. On February 9, 2026, the Council voted to approve items 40–43, which authorize Mosaic Consulting Group ($900K implementation fee; ~$750K annual operating costs) to deploy UKG Pro payroll software using $2.5M from a previously approved $6.25M budget—no new funds requested (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00038, CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00132). 

However, this approval comes after months of public scrutiny. An external audit released in June 2025 documented significant oversight failures, unusual procurement waivers, and heavy reliance on external consultants (notably Ernst & Young) under the previous Walsh administration (CTX-NEWS-2025-00061). Council Member Williams publicly stated the body was "at a point that we need to move forward" while hoping the administration can "prove us right" (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00038)—code for cautious skepticism. The project targets July 2027 go-live, replacing a fragmented system where current payroll processes require AS400 screenshots to be manually printed, handwritten, and re-entered (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00118). The City's timekeeping system (SWC) has been rolling out since mid-2025 and reaches 70% live deployment, with water department fully live and parks near completion (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00119).

The core political risk is institutional credibility. The Council witnessed $15M in police/fire overtime go unanalyzed until recently (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00123), multiple contractor terminations (EY, ERP1, Dear Alien Bait, Cherro Technologies), and a whistleblower complaint alleging EY used Advanced IT as a pass-through vendor (CTX-NEWS-2025-00038). The Finance Commissioner role is currently vacant, with Mayor Owens conducting a search (CTX-WEB-2026-00007), creating continuity risk on fiscal stewardship.

## Recent Council Activity

**February 3, 2026 – Finance Committee Briefing (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00118 through CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00124)**

The Finance Committee held a comprehensive payroll modernization presentation led by Josh Syrson (API project manager), the CFO, and Mike Smith (Assistant Director HR). Key objectives presented:

- **Current pain point:** Employee status changes (e.g., address update, tax withholding) require printing AS400 screenshot, hand-annotating changes, scanning, emailing to payroll, and manual re-entry—no direct employee self-service portal.
- **SWC timekeeping status:** 70% live across city (TeleStaff for public safety, SWC for civilian). Water fully live; Parks near-complete. Phased rollout at ~250 employees per month to manage support bandwidth.
- **System architecture:** SWC end-of-life March 2027; UKG Pro selected over Workday (would require scrapping SWC timekeeping and retraining entire organization). County server dependency adds failure points.
- **Staffing impact:** New system will not reduce headcount but redirect effort from manual compilation to analytics (e.g., identifying overtime spikes rather than just processing checks). Applicant tracking entirely paper-based; city lost Indeed full suite access (reduced from unlimited to 3 job postings/month with 90 open vacancies).
