## Executive Summary

The City of Syracuse is managing a complex federal housing portfolio centered on HUD Community Development Block Grant (CDBG), Emergency Solutions Grant (ESG), and HOME entitlement programs for fiscal year 2026 (Year 52). The Department of Neighborhood and Business Development (NBD) is executing the annual action plan with estimated entitlement funding in the low millions across all three programs, following a public hearing in February 2026 that drew modest participation (~15 attendees, 10 speakers). The critical institutional challenge is governance and execution capacity at the Syracuse Housing Authority (SHA), which oversees major capital projects but has experienced repeated leadership crises, open meetings law violations, and project delays that threaten the city's ability to deploy federal funds efficiently. The administration is simultaneously advancing two major housing transformation initiatives—the $50 million HUD Choice Neighborhoods East Adams project (Phase 2 remediation underway, deadline extended to September 2027) and the $220 million Parkside Commons renovation in the 15th Ward—while also responding to congressional funding cancellations and managing tenant relocation impacts from I-81 viaduct removal.

The CIO should monitor three operational risk zones: (1) SHA governance and execution capacity, which directly affects federal grant deployment; (2) HUD compliance procedures, particularly the new environmental review officer designation requirement; and (3) political volatility around affordable housing commitments, evidenced by deep council skepticism about developer promises and residents' lived experience of decades of broken housing pledges.

## Recent News Coverage

**SHA Governance Crisis (April–May 2025)**
The Syracuse Housing Authority experienced a cascading crisis documented by NBC3's I-Team: Executive Director Bill Simmons refused resignation demands despite agency problems; the board held secret sessions violating New York State Open Meetings Law; Board Chair Calvin Corriders Sr. later acknowledged the violation and committed to state self-reporting. Corriders departed his chair position in October 2025. (CTX-NEWS-2025-00009, CTX-NEWS-2025-00012, CTX-NEWS-2025-00018, CTX-NEWS-2025-00022, CTX-NEWS-2025-00028, CTX-NEWS-2025-00384)

**East Adams Project Delays and Governance Failures (July 2025)**
The East Adams transformation project received an 18-month deadline extension from the Planning Commission, pushing completion from Spring 2026 to September 2027. City officials publicly stated SHA leadership was "shooting holes in their own boat" by refusing to cooperate on $2 million environmental testing funding. These public conflicts between city and SHA preceded project advancement. (CTX-NEWS-2025-00081, CTX-NEWS-2025-00298)

**Federal Funding Cancellation (August 2025)**

**Project Progress Despite Governance Issues (October–November 2025)**

**Children Rising Center Project Stalled (July 2025)**
