## Executive Summary

Parkside Commons, a $220M mixed-income housing redevelopment anchored by BFC Partners (NYC) and SAEVI (Buffalo), remains the Council's highest-scrutiny project despite developer progress. The 393-unit project—splitting 200 west campus apartments for renovation and 193 east campus units for demolition/replacement—has been held repeatedly (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00105, CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00138) on tax exemption and shelter rent items since January 22. Core blockers are financial transparency demands from Councilors Williams and Paniagua (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00080, CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00135) and council-mandated community engagement exceeding developer initial offers (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00044, CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00046, CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00047). Council has explicitly invoked the East Adams redevelopment standard—demanding "genuine engagement" to reverse "decades and decades of broken housing promises" in the Parkside corridor (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00047). Developers have demonstrated material progress: 351 security cameras operational, full-time security director hired, 11+ squatter evictions assisted with SPD, and Section 8 protections documented (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00042). A $45K city land sale for Phase 1 construction (2016-2024 Fayette St East) was approved (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00074), and site plan details show clear demolition/replacement timeline. However, shelter rent and tax exemption votes remain held as of late February, indicating council is conditioning approval on financial clarity and sustained community engagement compliance.
