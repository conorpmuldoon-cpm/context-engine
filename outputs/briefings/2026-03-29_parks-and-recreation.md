## Executive Summary

Parks and Recreation operates within a constrained fiscal environment following May 2025 budget cuts that reduced the department's appropriation by 7% ($16M citywide cuts approved by Common Council). Mayor Walsh vetoed most cuts but the department still absorbed impacts. Despite budget pressure, the department is advancing a significant capital agenda: a $3M Inner Harbor railing project tied to I-81 displacement relief, a $500K inclusive playground, Creekwalk Phase 3 extension, Reconnecting Communities corridor improvements, and the Elmwood Park Furnace Brook restoration (FEMA-funded, running through October 2025). Commissioner Aisha Bird and Parks Commissioner Syeisha Byrd (in earlier capacity) have overseen grant awards and community programming expansion. However, the department faces ongoing safety scrutiny following a fatal shooting at Upper Onondaga Park Pool on June 25, 2025, prompting mayoral commitment to increased police presence at city pools. Battery recycling and e-sports programming represent emerging service lines, though council has raised safety and staffing concerns about kiosk placement at youth-focused community centers.
