## Executive Summary

Bridge strikes on the Onondaga Lake Parkway and Park Street CSX railroad bridge have emerged as a measurable public safety and infrastructure challenge in Syracuse during 2025–2026. The city has moved from reactive response to a coordinated, data-driven mitigation strategy centered on the installation of a "clanker system"—a mechanical warning device using suspended rubber buoys to alert oversized truck drivers before impact. This $500,000 project, fully reimbursable through New York State's CHIPS (Community & Infrastructure Planning) program, represents a best-practice adoption from Delaware and is championed by Chief Innovation Officer leadership. Concurrently, New York State Department of Transportation has launched a multi-state "Check Your Height, Know It's Right" public awareness campaign combining humorous video content with increased State Police enforcement. The issue carries political alignment signals from multiple departments and demonstrates coordination between city, state, and CSX Transportation stakeholders.

The initiative gained momentum following Mayor Ben Walsh's August 1, 2025 proposal for clanker devices, two days after a box truck struck the Parkway Bridge and closed all traffic lanes. City Engineer and COO leadership have since secured full state reimbursement, with installation targeted for mid-2026. This represents a shift from infrastructure damage response toward prevention infrastructure—a positioning that aligns with the city's Vision Zero framework and demonstrates the CIO office's role in identifying and championing scalable safety innovations.
