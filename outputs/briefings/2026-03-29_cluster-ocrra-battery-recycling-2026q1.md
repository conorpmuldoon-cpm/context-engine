## Executive Summary

The City of Syracuse is implementing an OCRRA (Onondaga County Resource Recovery Agency) battery recycling program funded by a Department of Energy grant, with kiosks planned for four community center sites: Southwest, Northeast, Cecil, and Magnarelli centers. The program expanded from suburban locations into the city in early 2026 and represents a partnership between Parks Department and OCRRA to manage spent batteries at community gathering points.

However, the Common Council has placed significant scrutiny on the initiative due to staffing and youth safety concerns at the proposed locations. Council members flagged that community centers operate with minimal staff (2 per site) and questioned whether youth-focused facilities are appropriate locations for battery recycling infrastructure. Fire safety protocols exist (kiosks use fire-protective materials with a 20-year zero-incident track record at Wegmans), but Council held the agreement through mid-January pending resolution of operational and liability questions. The DOE grant deadline created time pressure, but Council prioritized safety deliberation over expedited approval.

**Current Status (as of late February 2026):** The program appears to have moved forward after the Council's safety review, with Parks Commissioner presenting expansion to four sites as completed by early February. However, no vote record is provided in the dataset, suggesting either the item was approved in committee or remains in advanced stages of implementation.
