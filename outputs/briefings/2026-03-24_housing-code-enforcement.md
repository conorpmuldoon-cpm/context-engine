## Recent Council Activity

### Parkside Commons (CLUSTER-PARKSIDE-COMMONS-2026Q1 — 17 records)

The dominant council story of Q1 2026. BFC Partners (NYC, 40 years, 20,000 units) and SAEVI (Buffalo, founded 1977, first NYS LIHTC deal, 4,500 units) acquired the 393-unit Section 8 complex in October 2024 and presented a full redevelopment plan to the Economic Development Committee on January 22, 2026.

**Project scope:**
- **Total cost:** $220M ($125M direct construction)
- **Capital stack:** $75M from NYS Homes & Community Renewal (tax-exempt bonds + subsidy), ~$100M from LIHTC, ~$45M non-cash
- **West campus:** 200 units, full renovation (kitchens, bathrooms, plumbing/electrical, roofs, windows, electric heat pump HVAC)
- **East campus:** 193 units, demolition and replacement with 2 new buildings (Building A: 5 stories; Building B: 4 stories)
- **Unit count:** 393 total, one-for-one replacement per HUD requirements

**Council scrutiny themes:**
1. **Maintenance credibility** — Council directly contradicted developer claims about response times based on constituent experience. Work orders reduced from 1,200 to 185; staff doubled from 6 to 12.
2. **Security** — 351 cameras installed, 24/7 security team, but doors were UNLOCKED pending electronic fob installation. 20 units recovered from squatters/drug traffickers.
3. **Financial transparency** — Shelter rent figures debated: $215K (agenda), $280K (developer calculation), $800K (projected). City proposed $550/unit/year floor. Current shelter rent yields $270–330K/year; new PILOT projected at ~$800K/year.
4. **Community engagement** — Council invoked the East Adams standard and required 3+ additional community meetings. "Second biggest housing transformation in our city."
5. **Green space** — Councilor Jones-Rowser pressed for preservation commitments.
6. **East campus timeline** — At least 2 years out; council wants concrete plans.

**Hold pattern:** The shelter rent item was held at every session from January 22 through at least March 18 — an unusually long deliberation period signaling deep institutional skepticism. As of March 18, an appraiser was being hired and a vacant lot sale ($45K) to the developers was advancing, suggesting incremental progress.

**Key players:** Commissioner Michael Collins (NBD), Councilor Jimmy Monto (District 5, Economic Development Committee), Councilor Corey J. Williams (District 3, financial questions), Council President Rita Paniagua, Councilor Patrona Jones-Rowser (District 4), Assessment Commissioner Matt OJ, Deputy Commissioner Michael Layman.
