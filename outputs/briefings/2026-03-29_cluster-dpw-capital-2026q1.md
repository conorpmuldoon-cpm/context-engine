## Executive Summary

The Department of Public Works is executing a substantial capital improvement program in Q1 2026, anchored by $4M in CHIPS-funded salt barn replacement, multiple TIP (Transportation Improvement Program) projects, and Vision Zero infrastructure work. As of mid-March 2026, the administration has secured council approval for bond authorizations totaling approximately $15M+ across DPW facilities, roads, bridges, and fleet equipment. However, the capital program is experiencing cost overruns on key projects—Midland Avenue paving ($620K overrun), signal interconnect work ($260K overrun)—driven by bid delays from state DOT staffing shortages and supply chain constraints. Council is actively scrutinizing itemization and grant reimbursement processes, with the FY2025 independent audit flagging capital project tracking as a material weakness (repeat finding). The administration is also managing competing infrastructure priorities: Inner Harbor waterfront investment ($3M railings), SU campus traffic conversion requests, and broader Vision Zero corridor work (Colvin, Taylor, MLK, Adams, Harrison streets). Fiscal headwinds are present—ARPA funds are essentially exhausted, and the city faces a post-federal revenue cliff—but Q1 momentum suggests the council remains committed to infrastructure investment as an economic development priority.
