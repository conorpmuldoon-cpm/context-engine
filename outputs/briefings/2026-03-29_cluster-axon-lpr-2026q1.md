## Executive Summary

The City of Syracuse is navigating a contentious decision on license plate reader (LPR) technology deployment. In February 2026, the Common Council approved a contract with Axon for 26 LPR cameras after months of scrutiny focused on data privacy, federal immigration enforcement access, and data breach liability (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00039, CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00040). The contract expansion doubles the existing fleet from 13 to 26 cameras deployed across all city quadrants at traffic convergence points.

Council approval came despite sustained opposition from two councilors—Jimmy Monto and Corey Williams—who had sought to terminate an earlier Flock Safety contract ($250,000 for 26 cameras) citing immigration enforcement risks (CTX-NEWS-2025-00300). However, Councilor Monto publicly defended the Axon contract as "highly and easily revocable" with strong termination clauses if Axon violates terms, arguing the council had spent months negotiating protections (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00037). A parallel Flock camera ordinance was held pending resolution of the Axon contract (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00111). The Axon contract moved forward in early February 2026, though data breach liability terms were deferred to executive session—a detail that remains unresolved in public record (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00040).

Key tension: Privacy advocates and council members demanded guarantees that LPR data would not be shared with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE), while SPD emphasized that data ownership remains solely with the department in a closed system with no vendor access (CTX-COUNCIL-2026-00039). This briefing documents the political signals, outstanding questions, and governance risks as the deployment enters implementation phase.
