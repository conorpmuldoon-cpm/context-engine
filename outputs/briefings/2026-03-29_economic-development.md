## Executive Summary

Syracuse is navigating a complex economic development landscape dominated by the transformative $100 billion Micron Technology semiconductor factory investment in Clay, which is expected to generate 9,000 direct jobs plus 40,000+ supply chain positions and attract 50,000+ new residents to the region (CTX-NEWS-2025-00243). This influx creates unprecedented pressure on housing, infrastructure, and workforce development—but also reveals structural bottlenecks. The city has approved major projects including the Southside Renaissance Project ($41M mixed-use development, groundbreaking 2027), East Adams Phase One (133-unit affordable housing, opening 2027), and Syracuse University's three-building sophomore housing initiative ($360M combined, Comstock phase now under construction with unprecedented 2-year road closure). However, critical delays and partnership disruptions are undermining execution: the LaFayette Hills Estates housing development supporting Micron's workforce faces partnership collapse after the land owner withdrew (CTX-NEWS-2025-00216); City Hall Commons mixed-income conversion has stalled two years post-approval (CTX-NEWS-2025-00309); and housing construction remains at 25-year lows despite surging demand (CTX-NEWS-2025-00278). Council is closely scrutinizing development processes, demanding transparency on project economics, genuine community engagement, and equitable procurement. The administration faces a strategic credibility challenge: KeyBank CEO publicly predicted rapid regional growth (CTX-NEWS-2025-00243), but the city's ability to deliver housing, infrastructure, and workforce readiness at scale remains unproven.

**Housing Crisis Despite Demand Surge (Critical Sentiment)**
Investigative journalism revealed Syracuse-area housing construction has slumped to 25-year lows (588 units in 2024, only 218 permits through July 2025) despite surging demand and the incoming Micron project (CTX-NEWS-2025-00278). County officials cite a pipeline of ~16,000 proposed units, but most are stalled in permitting or financing. This represents a critical gap between economic opportunity and development capacity.

**Major Project Delays and Partnership Disruptions (Critical Sentiment)**
