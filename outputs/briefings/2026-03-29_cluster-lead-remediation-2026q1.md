## Executive Summary

Syracuse is executing a multi-year lead remediation strategy across water infrastructure and residential paint hazards, with mixed operational success and escalating community pressure. The Department of Water has demonstrated three consecutive quarters of EPA compliance (12 ppb vs. 15 ppb action level), positioning the city ahead of a November 2027 federal full-replacement mandate. However, approximately 14,000 lead service lines remain in the ground, and the city has completed only 3–12 homes in parallel brownfield/paint remediation efforts as of mid-2025—prompting public criticism of project delivery pace (CTX-NEWS-2025-00368).

DPW Commissioner Ehrenreich is championing a proactive full-replacement strategy (main-to-meter, not just public-side) and has secured $7M in capital funding ($2M federal grant for inventory, $5M bonded in 2025). Contractual execution challenges persist: the city transitioned testing vendors from 120 Water (emergency hire, community opposition) to Trupanti in Q1 2026, and split replacement work into smaller contractor packages to increase bidding competition. Concurrently, community organizations (Families for Lead Freedom, Earthjustice) are escalating pressure through EPA petitions, public testimony, and independent audits, arguing that the city's 2022 lead paint ordinance has generated 7,000+ citations without reducing childhood poisoning rates (CTX-NEWS-2025-00063).

A critical intelligence gap exists: the records show robust water compliance and capital planning, but sparse visibility into paint remediation execution, timeline-to-completion for 14,000+ service lines, or integration with the Chief Innovation Officer's broader equity and public health mandate.
