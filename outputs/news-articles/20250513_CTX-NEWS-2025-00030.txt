Syracuse, N.Y. — In eight years leading Syracuse, Mayor Ben Walsh said he's followed a couple of guidelines when it comes to his budgets: be conservative, plan for the worst-case scenario, and manage toward the best.

Mayor Walsh said he's proud of the fund balance strategy his administration has followed throughout the last eight years.

"When you look at where we started at around $50 million, and where we'll end at the end of this fiscal year at around $120 million, we've more than doubled the fund balance," he said.

Mayor Walsh added, "It's called a rainy day fund for a reason. And this year, it's raining for a number of reasons." He told the I-Team sales tax revenue, which is the city's biggest revenue sourced, is expected to level out soon. Plus, he said there's uncertainty about what federal funding cuts could be coming.

His $348 million spending plan proposed in this latest budget cycle also proposes taking $27 million out of the fund balance. He says that doesn't mean all of that will be spent.

"All but one budget since I took office, we anticipated fund balance draws, meaning a deficit. Four of those budgets we ended up with a surplus," said Mayor Walsh.

He showed the I-Team proof. Last year, they planned for a $19 million draw from the reserve fund, and only ended up needing $4.3 million.
