Syracuse, N.Y. — For
Pat Hogan
, the second time running for Syracuse mayor seemed different.
Unlike his losing primary campaign against then-Mayor Stephanie Miner in 2013, the common councilor had the official endorsement of the Onondaga County Democratic Committee in this year’s battle for the party’s nomination.
He also built a racially diverse coalition of elected city and state officials backing his effort, a healthy campaign fundraising apparatus and more name recognition after winning three more Syracuse Common Council races from 2019 through 2023 (giving him a total of seven council election victories).
But despite all of that, Hogan lost in Tuesday’s primary by a greater margin than his 2013 defeat, when he placed second in a three-way race with 28% of the vote.
In 2025, Hogan received just 23% of the Democratic primary vote, far below the 62% that gave
Sharon Owens
the nomination. Councilor
Chol Majok
placed third with 15%.
In an interview Thursday, Hogan told syracuse.com that he knew he was the underdog in the race, but he was surprised at the margin of victory.
Hogan, 75, was seeking to become city’s oldest mayor. His message in the primary campaign largely focused on criticisms of incumbent Mayor Ben Walsh’s administration, which he said an Owens administration would mirror.
In his concession speech Tuesday night, Hogan congratulated Owens and wished her well, but he also said he would “continue to fight for families, children and opportunities in our community. ... This work is not over. The need for compassion, principled, responsible and transparent government remains, and I’m not going anywhere.”
