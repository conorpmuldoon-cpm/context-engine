Syracuse Police discuss National Night Out and Dome Patrols

by Nicole La Fiandra

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The Syracuse Police Department is gearing up for National Night Out, an event designed to foster connections between the community and first responders. Scheduled for this Tuesday at Kirk Park, the event will feature SWAT equipment, firetrucks, food and music.

"It's just a great event for us to meet with the community on an informal level," said the Chief Joe Cecile of the Syracuse Police Department. The event aims to provide a relaxed atmosphere where residents can interact with law enforcement and other first responders.

In other news, Syracuse is preparing for the upcoming football season, which promises to draw large crowds to the Dome. The Syracuse Police Department has increased patrols to ensure safety both inside and outside the venue.

"We've increased the number of people we have on control also inside the dome itself," said the Chief. He advised attendees to plan ahead due to traffic limitations, noting, "Don't expect to leave your house 15 minutes before you want to arrive."
