SYRACUSE, N.Y. — A day after a 15-year-old was shot and killed outside Onondaga Park Pool, Syracuse families are expressing fear, frustration, and doubt that the city is doing enough to keep public spaces safe.

Mayor Ben Walsh released a statement following the shooting, promising increased police presence at pools across the city. But when our crew visited three pools on Wednesday, Kirk Park, Lincoln Park, and McKinley Park we didn’t see a single officer.

Several neighbors said they felt misled by the city’s promise.

“No. I wouldn’t have them down here. This pool ain’t safe,” said Dwayne Jefferson, a Syracuse parent.

Jefferson said he saw one patrol car near Kirk Park but claims the officer never got out.

“You saw one car sitting over there,” he said. “That dude did not get out of that car and walk around this pool. He stayed right in that car.”

The Onondaga Park Pool remains closed while police continue their investigation into Tuesday’s deadly shooting. Neighbors off camera said they haven’t noticed any increase in patrols since the incident and they’re worried.

“Nowadays you have to come to the park with your kids,” said Anthony Spratt, a Syracuse native. “It’s sad to say that.”

Bishop H. Bernard Alex, a longtime community activist, said the shooting has tainted a beloved public space that should never be associated with tragedy.

“We may have a couple of other hot days coming,” Alex said. “Are we going to see more empty pools? Empty parks because parents are afraid?”

Mayor Walsh’s office has not clarified when or where new patrols will begin. For now, families say they’re staying cautious and staying close until they see real changes.
