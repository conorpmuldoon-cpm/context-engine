Syracuse, N.Y. -- People hung from the top of a new chain-link fence at the end of Dickerson Street Wednesday morning.
The homeless staying at the Rescue Mission could see downtown Syracuse and the Salt City Market, but they couldn’t get there. The fence locked them in.
The street had been a well-traveled,
and troubled
, path between downtown and the Rescue Mission, Syracuse’s largest homeless shelter.
Workers came early Wednesday morning and put up a series of chain-link fences. They are locked to each other, and locked shut.
The move was something of a surprise to the Rescue Mission and its clients, whose view of downtown is now interrupted by the fence and a sign: Dead End.
Dickerson Street has been a source of trouble for years. The homeless and the people who prey on them gather there. Many struggle with mental illness. Most days, you can see drug sales, fights and overdoses. And as the city’s housing crisis has worsened, so have the troubles on the short street.
