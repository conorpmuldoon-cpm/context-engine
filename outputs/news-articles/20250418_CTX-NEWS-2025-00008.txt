Syracuse, N.Y. — Mable Wilson said the Syracuse Housing Authority Board of Commissioners needs to replace the agency's executive director, Bill Simmons.

Wilson called the I-Team Friday morning after seeing our reporting Thursday night. Simmons said, "I answer to the board. The board is the one that hires and fires me. Not the Corporation Counsel, not every resident in the housing."

"Bill Simmons said the most important thing yesterday. 'Not even the residents.' That's what we're living under," said Wilson. "It's a shame."

Wilson said the way he "reacted is showing just what we've been trying to tell the public is how we're being treated here at SHA as residents."

We first met Mable three months ago when the I-Team started filing reports on the agency and the paused Children Rising Center project.

Since then, Wilson has stayed in touch. "The board needs to step up and say, we're going to go in a new direction. We need to replace Bill Simmons and all the other entities and start over," she said.

The Mayor appoints five people to the SHA Board of Commissioners, and two public housing residents fill the other two seats. Mayor Walsh attended a recent board meeting, expressing a lack of confidence in SHA and their leader.

READ THE COMMENTS (14)

A spokesperson for Mayor Walsh told the I-Team: "Oversight of the Executive Director, including termination, is the responsibility of the SHA Board. Mayor Walsh has made his position clear regarding the need for a change in leadership, and he has continued to communicate with the Board regarding the performance of the Executive Director. Simultaneously, the Mayor has made his primary focus working to ensure progress occurs on the East Adams Transformation for the benefit of residents and the community."
