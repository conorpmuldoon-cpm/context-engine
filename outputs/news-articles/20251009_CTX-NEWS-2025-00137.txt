SYRACUSE, N.Y. — What was once the Syracuse Developmental Center will soon be turning into a residential space full of dozens of multi-income properties, if approved.

The property was originally seized by the City of Syracuse in 2019. This was after owing hundreds of thousands of dollars in taxes. In 2023, the City announced a new plan for the lot. Demolition of the space started in May 2024.

The property was bought by the Albanese Organization and will soon be transformed.

Most of the demolition of the area has been done, with the remains set to be finished by the end of October, 2025.

If approved, phase one of the project would consist of turning the lot into two-multi-family housing apartment buildings and 27 town homes. Housing prices will range from affordable to market rate, making this a multi-income development.

The market rate housing will consist of more town homes and senior housing. This will be a new neighborhood full of new roads, infrastructure and utilities. The last portion of the project until it gets approved is the financial aspect.

Eric Ennis, Deputy Commissioner in Syracuse's Department of Neighborhood and Business Development, said, "We're working with the Common Council on that."

"That would include local contribution as match funding," said Ennis. "In order for the project to make sure they have all the financing in place they need to move forward."
