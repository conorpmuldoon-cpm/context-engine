TOWN OF SALINA, N.Y. (WSYR-TV) — You ask, we answer!

The Your Stories Team has an update on the dispute to repair a short stretch of road that sits right on the DeWitt and Salina border.

The Town of Salina has closed a section of Townline Road.

Orange barrels now block drivers from using the popular cut-through that connects Route 298 and New Court Ave (Route 598).

The Your Stories Team first reported on the rough road back in December. Viewers were complaining that the pavement was in such disrepair, they were worried about cars being damaged.

The bumpy stretch of Townline Road in question was less than a tenth of a mile in length.

Those who drove the short stretch wanted to see it fixed. The problem we uncovered: Who’s responsible for making the repairs?

The Town of DeWitt Highway Department said they’re not responsible for Townline Road.

Town of Salina Supervisor Nick Paro, said the stretch of Townline had always been maintained by the NYSDOT because it’s wedged between two state routes.

However, the NYSDOT said Salina controls the road.

“The New York State Department of Transportation has periodically performed minimal maintenance, such as filling potholes, on the section of Townline Road south of State Route 298 and north of State Route 598. However, the roadway was and is controlled by the Town of Salina,” The NYSDOT said in a statement issued back in December.

Paro warned, if the state continued to claim it was Salina’s responsibility, the town would close the road and abandon it.

What’s next for Townline?

Now that Salina has closed it and abandoned the road, Paro said they’ll either allow the local glass repair shop located on Townline to use it as a private drive or they’ll turn it into greenspace.

NewsChannel 9 will update you when we learn more.
