Syracuse, N.Y. — Syracuse lawmakers want to hear from the public about the city police department’s plan to use unmanned aerial drones as a first responder to 911 calls.
The Syracuse Common Council on Monday unanimously approved advertising a public hearing on the drone program at 5:30 p.m. Sept. 15 in the council chambers of City Hall. Councilor Chol Majok, who chairs the council’s Public Safety Committee, said concerns about privacy and data sharing have councilors taking a cautious approach to approving a contract that would allow the program to launch.
“We’d rather put this in the hands of the community and hear from the community, who are to be most affected by this,” Majok said Monday.
The hearing will be city government’s second formal call for resident input. In January, Mayor Ben Walsh’s Surveillance Technology Working Group — which reviews potential use of technology that may raise privacy concerns — gathered feedback that was mostly opposed to the technology. The working group eventually approved the technology’s use, and the police department sought the council’s authorization on a key contract
in the spring
.
SPD leaders have said the drones would
improve response times
and provide important information to officers before they arrive at scenes. They’ve made comparisons to the city’s surveillance cameras on street corners, which initially raised similar privacy concerns but have become something many residents seek for their neighborhoods as a crime deterrent.
The Common Council approved spending $250,000 in November for the drone equipment, but councilors held off voting on a five-year, $57,000 licensing contract with Axon Enterprises Inc. that would provide FAA clearance for remote drone operation.
More information on the drone program can be found at
www.syr.gov/surveillancetech
.
