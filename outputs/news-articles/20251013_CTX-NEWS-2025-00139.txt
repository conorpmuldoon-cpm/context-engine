Syracuse, N.Y. — Five years ago, on Columbus or Indigenous Peoples' Day in Syracuse, Mayor Ben Walsh announced his plans to remove and relocate the statue of Christopher Columbus. He called for the creation of a new space, that would be called Heritage Park.

An advisory committee made up of community stakeholders falling on all different sides of the debate was formed.

The Columbus Monument Corporation took the city to court, and despite the back-and-forth battle, a judge ultimately denied the group's final appeal in June 2024.

That decision determined that the city has the right and authority to move the statue elsewhere. But since then, it's been in the same spot.

"It will be up through my term as mayor. I'm still hopeful that we can continue that process forward," said Mayor Walsh last week when CNY Central asked for an update.

His Deputy Mayor, Sharon Owens, is running to lead the city next. She said, "We'll follow through on the process that we're in now to take a look at the Heritage Park process to make a decision. In 2026 that'll be a priority for me."

The I-Team asked former Onondaga County Executive and Columbus Monument Corporation member Nick Pirro for his reaction to that.

'This statue is staying here, and so are we': pushback on plans to remove Columbus statue

"We offered to help support, create the Heritage Park here. Just leave the statue," said Pirro. He added, "The courts ruled that we were premature in our lawsuit. Legal battles remain out there if we need to. We're hoping that whatever the new administration's going to be, it will work with us to resolve this issue."
