Syracuse police chief clarifies policy adherence after PBA posts

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Syracuse Police Chief Joe Cecile addressed recent tensions between the police department and the Police Benevolent Association (PBA) following a controversial post by PBA President Joe Moran. The original post, written by Moran, said "perpetrators and gangbangers have infiltrated the bar scene on Friday and Saturday nights." The post that followed suggested that officers should disregard departmental policies in Armory Square, an area reportedly infiltrated with crime. "De-escalation is out the window," the second post read. De-escalation is a notable part of SPD's policy.

Chief Cecile emphasized that Moran does not have the authority to direct officers on policy. "Our professional officers are not going to deviate from what they've done in the past, which is follow policies or procedures and get the job done," said the Chief. He clarified that the PBA President does not write or train officers on policy, stressing the importance of following established procedures.

The Chief also noted that Moran has since walked back some of his comments. "I think he even realizes he went a little too far, went over skis a bit ... You can't tell officers to disregard positive procedures."

The Chief praised the department's officers for their professionalism, particularly during this past weekend in Armory Square. "The officers are very proactive, very professional, and those arrests set the tone so that we didn't have the large block party setting up and deciding to party until 5 a.m.," he said.

Chief Cecile acknowledged the influence of the PBA President but reiterated the importance of intentional leadership. "Whether you have influence or rank, you have to be very intentional about what you say to people," he said.

The Chief assured that the situation has calmed down and that discussions with Moran will continue.
