SYRACUSE, N.Y. (WSYR-TV)– Syracuse’s carbon footprint dropped by a wide margin last year compared to 2010, on both municipal and residential levels, according to a new report.
The city’s
Greenhouse Gas Emission Inventory (GHGI) for 2024
, released Thursday, July 24, shows that over a 14-year period, Syracuse’s municipal government reduced its carbon emissions by 67%, and the greater Syracuse community reduced its emissions by 29%.
Both numbers significantly exceeded the city’s goals from 2010. The GHGI set a goal of reducing municipal emissions by 40% and community emissions by 7%.
