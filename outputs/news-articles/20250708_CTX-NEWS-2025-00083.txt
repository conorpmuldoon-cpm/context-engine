Latest construction update for STEAM School in Syracuse reveals plan to create bus loop

by Brandon Ladd

Tue, July 8, 2025 at 4:22 AM

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — There's new progress in opening the STEAM High School in Syracuse.

City leaders have requested to permanently close the section of South Warren Street in front of the school, in order to use it for busses each school day.

Syracuse Common Council will hear their plans at a later date, which will need input from the State Dept. of Transportation as well.

Syracuse City School District leaders went before a council committee late last month and said the school would open its doors to 250 ninth grade students in September for the first day of school.
