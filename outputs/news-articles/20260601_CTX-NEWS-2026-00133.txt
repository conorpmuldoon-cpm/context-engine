DeWitt, N.Y. — Three summers ago, Micron Technology mounted microphones on 10-foot-tall poles in a forest in Clay. Over 17 nights, those microphones recorded the squeaks and chirps of a cauldron of bats. The results confirmed what everyone had suspected: The site where one of the world’s largest chipmakers proposed to build its flagship U.S. factory was a maternity roost for the Indiana bat, an endangered species.
