SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Listings are now available for Tower701 apartments on East Genesee Street, a building that was formerly the Crowne Plaza hotel before closing to guests in Fall 2024. The revamped structure is now aimed at young professionals.

Syracuse Deputy Mayor Sharon Owens commented on the development's role in addressing housing needs.

"It meets the needs of some of the housing crisis. I don't anticipate that building solving the housing crisis for us at all, but it would meet the need of young professionals who are in entry-level positions and corporations or in companies in our community," she said.

Tower701 has released renderings of its rooms, revealing that a 315-square-foot studio apartment will cost $1,850 per month.

Tower 701 renderings, studio apartment (Credit: Tower 701).

Additional fees include a $20 application fee, pet fees, parking fees, and a fee for additional occupants. The units do not feature a full kitchen, but a communal kitchen is available on the 20th floor.

Tower 701 renderings, common space (Credit: Tower 701).

The rent at Tower701 exceeds Syracuse's average price for a one-bedroom apartment, which is $1,230 per month according to a February report from Zumper.

Local resident Isaac Dinton expressed skepticism about the value to CNY Central.

"There is a communal kitchen that doesn't really sound too appealing," said Dinton. "Especially for $1,800 in what is a studio apartment. I think a lot of people probably expect a little bit more for that price."

Zumper reported that Syracuse ranks as the 65th most expensive rental market in the country, with Apartments.com noting that the city is 2.7% more expensive than the national average for renters.

Tower701 aims to help renters "budget with ease," offering fully furnished units with Wi-Fi, a 55-inch smart TV, and included utilities. Property managers noted that 12% of the units will be designated as affordable housing.

Tower 701 renderings, studio apartment (Credit: Tower 701).

Owens emphasized the need for a strategic approach to the housing crisis.

"We absolutely have to have more housing," said Owens. "It is a chess game of the type of housing and the quantity of housing downtown and neighborhoods throughout the city that are being built."
