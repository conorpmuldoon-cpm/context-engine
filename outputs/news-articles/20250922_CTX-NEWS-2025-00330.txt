Syracuse, N.Y. — More than 350 housing units will be ready to rent next spring under the terms of a lawsuit settlement between the city of Syracuse and the owner of a notorious apartment building.
The city and Chicago-based Clear Investment Group have agreed to settle a lawsuit the city
filed in April
that sought to have a receiver take over operation of the formerly named Skyline Apartments on James Street.
City officials claimed the owner was continuing neglect of the property it acquired in 2023 in a court-ordered sale of four city properties by
Tim and Troy Green
. Clear Investment said that was
not the case
and blamed the city for slowing down their ability to complete renovations.
Under the terms of settlement filed Friday in state Supreme Court in Onondaga County, Clear Investment will complete renovation work and have apartment units ready for occupancy in multiple phases over the next seven months.
The city will drop its effort to bring in a court-appointed receiver and will issue partial certificates of occupancy to allow portions of the building to open as renovations of specific floors or wings are completed.
Clear also agreed to allow the city to conduct weekly inspections of the building to monitor progress.
“Syracuse needs Clear to deliver on its promise to restore Skyline Apartments to a quality and affordable place to live,” Mayor Ben Walsh said. “This agreement lays out an aggressive and achievable plan for progress with accountability on Clear to get those critically important units back online as soon as possible.”
Clear officials could not immediately be reached for comment.
In the two years after buying the Skyline, Clear Investment vacated the 12-story building to do a full-scale renovation. But a fire and a massive water leak caused substantial damage to the building, and it has not been deemed fit for habitation.
Clear was itself a receiver in the litigation against the Greens, and it bought the Skyline for $13 million after proposing a $4.6 million
renovation plan
. Clear Investment plans to change the building’s name to The Metropolitan.
Under the Greens, the Skyline was notorious for unsanitary conditions, code violations and crime. A group of former tenants recently secured an
$800,000
class action settlement in a lawsuit they filed against the Greens.
