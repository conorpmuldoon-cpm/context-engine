SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The SUNY College of Environmental Science and Forestry will honor Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh and attorney Phil Bousquet with the 2025 Sol Feinstone Environmental Award for their contributions to environmental stewardship and community service.

The award recognizes individuals who care for the environment, promote volunteerism, and advance public understanding of environmental issues.

Bousquet, founding member of Bousquet Holstein PLLC, is a leading authority on New York’s Brownfield Cleanup Program, which helps redevelop contaminated sites. His work has supported more than $9 billion in construction value and $1.5 billion in tax credits across the state.

Walsh, Syracuse’s 54th and first independent mayor, has focused on sustainability and inclusive growth since taking office in 2018. His administration launched the Sustainable Syracuse Initiative and the city’s Climate Action Plan, emphasizing clean energy, workforce development, and affordable housing.

ESF President Joanie Mahoney said both honorees “embody the Feinstone spirit through their dedication to environmental progress and community service.”
