SYRACUSE, N.Y. — UPDATE:

We asked the Syracuse Police Department why crime is down, even though there is still a trend of violence in the city. Sgt. Thomas Blake says the lowered numbers still reflect individual incidents we see from day to day. "I think the data supports that. You're still getting those cases, and certainly when something like that comes out it is a huge tragedy. And it is going to get amplified, everyone is going to hear about it. The numbers are what they are. There's a significant decrease over the past year: Homicides are down 25%, burglaries are down 30%, stolen vehicles down 51%."

ORIGINAL STORY:

The latest COMPSTAT data from the City of Syracuse shows reduced crime in 2025.

Mayor Ben Walsh and Police Chief Joe Cecile announced that crime is down 27.3% through the first seven months of this year. Violent offenses are down 18% and property crime is down 29.2%.

Soggy Sunday in CNY, spring warmth back to winter chill and snowflakes

The COMPSTAT report said crime also down compared to the five-year average. According to Walsh and Cecile, this is a reliable indicator of public safety trends. Overall crime is down 11.3%, violent crime is down 25.7% and property crime is down 7.1% when compared to that measure.

The COMPSTAT data also showed reduced gun crimes for this year so far. The report that shots fired are down 22%, shootings with injuries are down 3%, and shooting victims injured or killed is down 4%.
