Syracuse, N.Y. – Armory Square sits at the center of Syracuse nightlife – a two-block strip of restaurants, bars and a late-night pizza spot that attracts people from across the region. It’s a lively neighborhood where people gather to drink, dance and hang out with friends.
In recent weeks, however, the neighborhood found itself at the center of a heated community debate about safety, policing, crime and late-night shenanigans.
Police and city officials have spent weeks trading barbs after a
Facebook post
went Syracuse “viral” this month with over 600 likes and 360 shares. In the post, the police union president said “gangbangers” have infiltrated the square. It described a chaotic, violent scene.
The police chief, deputy mayor and others
balked at the union’s description
. They said problems began after business hours, when people would gather in large groups after bars closed at 2 a.m.
We decided to see for ourselves what Armory Square is like on a weekend night.
Two syracuse.com reporters visited the square for four hours on Friday, July 18.
