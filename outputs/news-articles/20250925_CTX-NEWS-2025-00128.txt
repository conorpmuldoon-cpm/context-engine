Clear Investment Group pledges to transform troubled Skyline Apartments in Syracuse

by Matthew Benninger

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Clear Investment Group said there is "much to work to be done" when it comes to renovating the incredibly dilapidated Skyline Apartments in Syracuse.

Clear released a statement on Thursday, promising to repair and restore the James Street property that became well known for a myriad of issues. Those issues include a 2021 murder, drug activity, flooding, a fire and vermin.

The group took over the property from Green Skyline Apartments, LLC in 2023 and promised to transform it under the name "The Metropolitan." Clear said that the problems it "inherited" were the fault of the previous owner.

“Our priority has always been to create safe, respectable communities for our residents,” said CEO of Clear Investment Group Amy Rubenstein. “While we did not cause the problems that led to this settlement, we accept the responsibility of addressing them. We are committed to working collaboratively to ensure Metropolitan is a place residents are proud to call home.”

Clear said it's continuing to work with the City of Syracuse and local officials to ascertain that the building is habitable.

On Sept. 22, Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh announced that the city reached an agreement that commits Clear to fix the property for future tenants. In early April, Mayor Walsh announced that the city was taking legal action against Clear for failing to address the building's issues.

The New York State Supreme Court approved the agreement on Sept. 19.

The court ruled that Clear has until April 30, 2026, to complete repairs. By that time, the building is expected to be ready for tenants to live safely.

Recently, Legal Services of Central New York announced that it has secured an $800,000 settlement on behalf of former tenants against Green Skyline Apartments, LLC. Clear said it was not involved in that lawsuit.
