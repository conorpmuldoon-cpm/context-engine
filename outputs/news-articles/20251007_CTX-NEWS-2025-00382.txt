Syracuse, N.Y. — Syracuse City School District Superintendent Anthony Davis' involvement in the district may continue well after he leaves his role on October 31.

According to public documents, the district is looking to hire a company called TD22 Consulting LLC, which is owned by the outgoing Superintendent.

The agenda for the September 24 board of education work session shows the company would be paid up to $125,000 to provide support and coaching to district employees.

The document also shows the goal would be "To demonstrate improvement in the implementation and achievement outcomes of the strategic plan and provide overall district transition support."

Pamela Odom, the current Deputy Superintendent will taken over for Davis on November 1.
