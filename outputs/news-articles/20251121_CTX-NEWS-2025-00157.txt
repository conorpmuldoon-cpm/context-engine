SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The vacant City Hall Commons in Syracuse could be getting a new lease on life.

The seven story tall building in Hanover Square at the intersection of East Genesee Street and East Washington Street is being eyed by a Syracuse Architecture Firm to be converted into 39 proposed mixed-use units.

The firm, Holmes King Kallquist & Associates, said it would want a restaurant to serve as the anchor tenant for the property.

The rest of the building would be used for housing.

The historic Syracuse building was built in 1869 and has been owned by the City of Syracuse in recent years.
