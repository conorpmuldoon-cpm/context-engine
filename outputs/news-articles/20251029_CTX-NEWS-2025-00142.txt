SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Syracuse city officials and Home Headquarters got a sneak peek Tuesday at new affordable housing is taking shape over at Syracuse's Tipperary Hill.

Part of New York State's Affordable Homeownership Opportunity Program (AHOP) and the city's Resurgent Neighborhood Initiative (RNI), the project will see four new single-family homes being built, with one of the houses representing the 26th house built by Home HeadQuarters.

"From the moment they got involved, things moved quickly," said Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh. "It's a team effort like it always is, but it started with the neighborhood advocating for itself. Then it was about getting the right housing partner involved and the right support from the city and New York State."

The homes will be available to purchase for income eligible buyers and will be Energy-Star rated as well as energy-efficient.

"Whether you're going to the Southside or the Westside, any part of the city, we're building homes," said Mayor Walsh.
