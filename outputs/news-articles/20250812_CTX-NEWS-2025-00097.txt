SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Stop lights and speeding zones may start to have cameras for this upcoming school year. This is part of the Automated School Zone Enforcement Program in the City of Syracuse.

The Common Council met on Monday to discuss the future of the project. The Council ended up holding off on that decision, because they felt they didn't have enough information.

If this project gets approved by the Common Council, there will be 34 locations with speed cameras and 36 locations or red light cameras. Although Common Councilor at Large, Chol Majok, said that eventually these numbers could expand to outside of school campuses.

Fines are set at $50 and construction is already underway.

Conor Muldoon, Chief Operating Officer for the City of Syracuse, said the costs of the project will not be coming out of taxpayers' pockets.

"All the costs for the infrastructure development of the system will come from a third-party vendor," said Muldoon.

Jenoptik is the vendor for the project and will be fronting the costs of construction and permits. They will then receive their investment back through a revenue share as part of the fines collected.

The program will start rolling out on Sept. 3 if approved by the council. Not all of the cameras will be ready by then, but the schedule of when has not yet been released.
