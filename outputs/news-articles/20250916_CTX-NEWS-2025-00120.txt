SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Neighbors in Syracuse had a chance to speak out on the city's plans for a police drone program at a public hearing Monday.

If approved by the Common Council, the Syracuse Police Department would work with security company Axon Enterprises for a five-year drone program.

It would continue the department's business relationship with the company, which already supplies the department with body cameras.

Related article: Gov. Hochul touts $6.7 million in new police technology in Syracuse

Neighbors voiced concerns over the idea of having drones, citing lack of privacy and wondering how data from the program would be shared.

“Unrestricted use of drones by police is not acceptable,” said Lee Cridland of the Syracuse Peace Council. “Additionally, there's no way to protect our rights to public protest. There needs to be safeguards in place that prohibit the use of drones in public demonstrations and other gatherings. Surveillance of private citizens is a violation of our 4th amendment rights and marks a disturbing trend.
