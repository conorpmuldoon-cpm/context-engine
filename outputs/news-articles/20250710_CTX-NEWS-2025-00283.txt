Syracuse, N.Y. — A 15-year-old charged with
killing another teen at the Upper Onondaga Park Pool
was on probation at the time of the fatal shooting.
Kyari Cunningham
, of Syracuse, is charged with shooting
A’Dhon Thomas
once on June 24 around 2 p.m. just outside the pool’s fence as dozens of families swam inside, according to a criminal complaint.
Thomas was taken to Upstate University Hospital, where he later died.
On Thursday, Cunningham was arraigned on a violation of probation in front of Judge Matthew Doran.
Cunningham is accused of violating probation by being charged with the homicide, prosecutor Anthony Copani said. He was arrested Monday and is currently being housed at the Hillbrook Detention Center.
Copani could not share why Cunningham was serving probation because he was adjudicated as a youthful offender and the case was sealed, he said.
Cunningham is now charged with second-degree murder and second-degree criminal possession of a weapon. He has not yet been indicted on the new charges.
Mayor Ben Walsh has
said
there will be a larger police presence this summer around city pools.
