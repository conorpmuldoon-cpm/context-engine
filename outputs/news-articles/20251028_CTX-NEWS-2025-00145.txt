Syracuse to launch parking amnesty period in November, late fees to be waived

by Brandon Ladd

Tue, October 28, 2025 at 6:27 AM
Updated Mon, November 3, 2025 at 7:25 AM

SYRACUSE, N.Y — The City of Syracuse is offering drivers a chance to pay back late parking tickets at less cost than usual.

Syracuse is offering an amnesty period, trying to collect back some of the money 'owed' to the city, from Nov. 1 to 31. The Finance, Taxation & Assessment Committee on the Syracuse Common Council discussed the topic on Oct. 27.

That means, if you own one of the 140,000 unpaid parking tickets in the City of Syracuse, including the 124,000 tickets from the past two decades, you could pay back a fraction of what you owe. Many of those tickets, because unpaid, would result in drivers' vehicles having a boot put on them if the city were to issue them another ticket.

Neighbors who pay back during the amnesty period would pay the price of their initial parking ticket and a $15 to $30 surcharge. Most parking tickets are $25 in Syracuse.

The combined monetary value of those tickets without late penalties/interest would total approximately $5,300,000. A similar initiative occurred in 2020. From September 8-25, 2020, about 34,000 parking tickets were cleared and the city collected $960,520.
