SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The Syracuse Police Department has reported a significant decrease in homicides, with a 30% drop compared to this time last year, according to the latest COMPSTAT data. The department attributes this decline to enhanced community outreach efforts and strategic policing.

CONTINUING COVERAGE | Shootings are up in Syracuse from last year, Community speaks on child-involved violence

"We're trying to get ahead of trends," said Chief Joseph Cecile of the Syracuse Police Department. "One of the trends that we have been looking at over the past couple of years is homicide. Right now, in that part one crime section, homicides are down 30%."

The department highlighted the role of outreach workers and community leaders, like Rev. Lateef Johnson-Kinsey, in encouraging individuals to resolve conflicts without resorting to gun violence. Johnson-Kinsey is the first Director of the Mayor's Office to Reduce Gun Violence. "Outreach workers going out there trying to get folks to put down the guns," said the Chief.

Despite the positive trend, the department remains cautious. "We certainly are not declaring success now and we just had the homicide at the pool, so still got a lot of lot of hard work to do, but we're going in a good direction," the Chief added.

In addition to the reduction in homicides, the department noted a 10% decrease in call volume. This decline is attributed to several factors, including the use of electronic reporting for insurance purposes and alternative responses to mental health incidents.
