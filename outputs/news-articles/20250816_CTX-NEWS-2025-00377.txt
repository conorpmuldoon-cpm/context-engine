Mary Nelson Youth Center hosts annual back-to-school giveaway

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — For more than two decades, the Mary Nelson Youth Center has been helping local families prepare for the school year, and this year is no different.

On Saturday, the center held its 2025 Back2School Supply Giveaway at Meachem Park, providing backpacks filled with supplies to children across the community. The event ran from 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.

The giveaway began in 2002, inspired by founder Mary Nelson’s nephew, Darryl Patterson, who died after being shot by his best friend in 2001. A month later, Nelson met a young man who was selling drugs to pay for college textbooks an encounter that inspired her to make a difference.

Since then, Nelson has hosted the annual event, which has grown into one of the community’s biggest back-to-school traditions.

Students of all ages were able to pick up supplies tailored to their needs:
Kindergarteners received crayons, glue sticks, scissors, and folders.
Middle schoolers were given pens, notebooks, binders, and calculators.
High schoolers walked away with planners, graphing calculators, and even laptops.

The goal is to ensure every child, regardless of financial situation, starts the school year ready to learn.

Nelson says the event would not be possible without the donations and volunteers who continue to rally behind the youth center. Each year, thousands of children benefit from the program.

“The need is always there,” Nelson said in past interviews. “If we can take the burden off parents and give kids the tools they need to succeed, then we’re doing our job.”

While backpacks and supplies were the focus, the event also brought the community together with food, music, and activities for families. Donations are still being accepted to support both the giveaway and year-round programming at the Mary Nelson Youth Center.
