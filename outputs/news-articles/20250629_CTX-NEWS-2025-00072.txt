Syracuse Police Chief discusses city pool safety after homicide

by Kaitlin Pearson

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Syracuse, N.Y. — Syracuse Police Department's Chief of Police Joseph Cecile answers law enforcement questions from viewers to increase transparency and community relations.

This week, Chief Cecile discusses new efforts to increase safety at city pools after a 15-year-old was shot and killed at Onondaga Park Pool last week.

Chief Cecile said they have put together a detail at the request of Mayor Ben Walsh that will involve officers roving from pool to pool. He said they will stop at the pool and speak to the lifeguard on duty about any concerns. They will oversee the parks where the pools are located as well.

He also said now that school is out, school resource officers will be available and stop by city pools at different times. He said this is a valuable resource because resource officers have a relationship with the kids.

The Chief also spoke about the arrest made in the shooting of three children on Hier Ave. He said even though there were no fatalities, the homicide unit was brought in to work on the case.
