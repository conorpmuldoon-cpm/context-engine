SYRACUSE, N.Y. — One out of every four dollars in the City of Syracuse budget goes to the Police and Fire Departments.

Between the two departments, the total is projected to hover around $100 million this year.

In separate hearings on Tuesday, April 22, Fire Chief Michael Monds and Police Chief Joseph Cecile both advocated for their budgets before members of the Syracuse Common Council.

Chief Cecile defended the pay his current staff is receiving and the retirement benefits of past police members. The Common Councilors agreed the already understaffed Police Department would not be able to find much savings in that section of the budget.

Instead, when posed with the question of long term savings, Chief Cecile mentioned expanding social workers responding to mental health emergency calls as a way to potentially reduce the department's costs.

Cecile cited the City of Eugene, Oregon, reducing calls their police members actually responded to by four percent after relying more on social workers and drones.

He also said the 10-hour shifts the Syracuse Police Department went to in recent years has helped with stabilizing overtime costs.

Chief Monds said the Syracuse Fire Department is the second busiest in New York State behind New York City. With fire personnel responding to almost 30,000 calls per year according to the proposed City of Syracuse budget.

The department is known for the speed it responds to calls, at times competing for the quick response time in the country.

Despite the department's accomplishments, Chief Monds argued for more firefighter positions in the next city budget. He said more training and on-scene personnel is needed to keep firefighters safe. He hopes the Common Council approves an additional $3 million for the department compared to last year.
