SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Inspired by the success of what's called a "clanker" system in Newark, Delaware, the City of Syracuse is planning to build its own in 2026.

It'll warn truck drivers and those with tall vehicles that they can't clear the height of the CSX-owned railroad bridge along Park Street in Syracuse.

Syracuse Police data obtained by the I-Team shows 42 bridge strikes since 2020. Soon, there will be a giant arm with hanging aquatic buoys over the road.

"Trucks that are too tall that would hit the bridge, they'll get that physical response immediately to say, that if you continue, you're going to have a much-worse problem on your hands," explained City of Syracuse COO Conor Muldoon. "So pull over, stop and turn around."

It's an idea Matheu Carter, a transportation engineer with the University of Delaware, said is working for their own problem railroad bridge in Newark on Casho Mill Road.

Dozens of strikes and failed attempts to get drivers to obey signs posted led them to get creative and look for a new way to thwart future strikes. The clanker system does that, along with a sign that says: "STOP NOW! Or KABOOM!"

"It's sort of a creative way of really capturing people's attention and saying this is something you've got to pay attention to," said Carter.

The solution has become an icon to people in the area, inspiring Halloween costumes and people even decorating their Christmas trees with ornaments of the clanker system. There's even a Facebook page dedicated to it.

Like the Casho Mill Road bridge, the Park Street Bridge has not seen a fatality from a strike. Muldoon said it's the hope that this new approach will keep it that way.
