SYRACUSE, N.Y. — For the first time, CNY Central cameras were invited inside Eastwood Heights.

Jerlene Harper, a newly retired Syracuse Housing Authority Board Commissioner, has lived there since 2011. She invited CNY Central for a tour.

She grabbed a dusting wand and swiped it a few times over an air vent in the hallway of the third floor, explaining that there's always lint and most people prefer to wear masks.

Then, she pointed out multiple spots on each floor where water damage was present.

"It's getting closer to the apartments now. We can tell where the leaks are by the stains in the ceiling," she said as she pointed out spots.

'Look at what we're living with': Eastwood Heights residents show conditions to the I-Team

The dirt and lint, and the leaking roof are two major concerns for Harper and her fellow senior residents.

But the project that's supposed to renovate their building keeps getting delayed. A third iteration of a deal between their landlord, the Syracuse Housing Authority and the city of Syracuse is now up for a Common Council vote in the coming weeks.

The construction was supposed to start after a vote in March, but more delays happened.

"All they're doing is sitting around," said Harper, "Having all these discussions and we're the ones that are suffering and scared that the apartment ceiling's going to be in the bed with us."

In the front lobby of the building, residents have to constantly walk by renderings on foam board of what their new complex is supposed to look like.

"They've cost so much stress and strain on us in here," said Birtha Truell, a resident. "They're telling us they're going to do one thing and they come back with a meeting and it's a different story. Now, we don't know what they're going to do."

Another resident told CNY Central, "This is supposed to be one of the 'elite' buildings, but look at what we're living with."

Jerlene Harper vowed to keep highlighting issues that are in need of immediate attention until something is done.
