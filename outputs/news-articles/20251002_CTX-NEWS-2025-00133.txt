How many traffic violations have been found in Syracuse's School Zone Enforcement program?

by Hannah Devanny

Thu, October 2, 2025 at 7:56 PM

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — 60,380 school zone traffic violations were recorded in the City of Syracuse during the first two weeks of a School Zone Enforcement Program.

Of the 60,380 violations, 59,950 were due to speeding. 430 of those were due to going through a red light. The highest recorded speed in and around a school zone so far is 78 miles per hour.

Those traffic violations were during the city's warning period. This means no fines or tickets will be given out. The warning period ends in one month, on November second.

Once the grace period is over, every right light camera and speed violation done, will be a $50 fine. This can go up to $75 if you pay late.

City leaders say the goal of the red light cameras and speedometers is not to generate money. Instead, it's to encourage safe driving and keep children safe.

Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh said “Syracuse schools are facing a speeding epidemic. We are seeing a concerning number of drivers exceeding the speed limit, running red lights and passing school buses where children are present. These are only warnings, so drivers have another month to change their driving behavior. So many things are out of our control, but safe driving is not one of them. We can lower these incidents to make our children, families and school staff safer.”
