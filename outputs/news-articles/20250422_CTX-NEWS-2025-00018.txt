Syracuse, N.Y. — The Syracuse Housing Authority Board is made up of five people appointed by Mayor Ben Walsh and two residents living in public housing run by SHA.

More than 20 reports filed by the I-team show a trail of failed leadership, dysfunction and concerns raised by Mayor Walsh and other members of his administration.

The Housing Authority is the lead agency working with other partners on the East Adams Neighborhood housing project, with tens of millions of dollars at stake.

Last Thursday, the board violated open meetings law by not allowing CNY Central journalists, the city's Neighborhood and Business Development Commissioner and Deputy Commissioner, and two other community members in until well after the meeting started.

Based on the I-Team's three months of reporting so far, we asked Bill Simmons, SHA's Executive Director, if he considered resigning or if anyone on the board has asked him to. He said, "The board hires me and fires me. The board has not come to me and said we want you to step down. So, that's all that counts."

On Friday, the I-Team emailed each of the seven commissioners, inviting them to participate and do an interview to introduce themselves.

The I-Team asked the following two questions:

Did you know the board violated New York State open meetings law by failing to allow the public and our crew in at the start of the Board meeting?
Bill Simmons says nobody on the board has asked him to step down. Is that true?

As of Tuesday afternoon, there were no responses back.

Christopher Montgomery -- Vice Chair
