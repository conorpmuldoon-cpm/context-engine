SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The Syracuse Common Council has unanimously rejected the city's proposed budget, prompting concerns from the Syracuse Police Department about potential impacts on public safety initiatives. The council hired an outside agency to analyze the budget and recommended cutting current spending.

MORE | Syracuse police chief addresses recent spike in violent crimes, memorial coming up

Police officials warned that the cuts could eliminate several key programs, including a noise party detail and aggressive panhandling initiatives downtown. These programs, implemented to maintain order in the "Salt City" area, rely heavily on overtime funding.

Additionally, plans to hire a new class of officers before the mayor leaves office are now uncertain.

"We would have to overlap classes again ... we use overtime to do that. That is now out the window," said Chief Cecile. The department is already facing a staffing shortage, having lost 10 officers this year with another 15 expected to retire.

Despite the cuts, major functions such as 911 calls, drug investigations, and major crimes will continue to be handled by the Syracuse Police Department. However, the loss of support for events like the St. Patrick's Day parade raises concerns about public safety during large gatherings.

The department emphasized the importance of tools like ShotSpotter, which alerts officers to potential gunfire.

"Sometimes, if there's no 911, that's the only alert you get," said the Chief, highlighting the tool's value in investigations.
