SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The Syracuse Fire Department is seeking a new spot to fix its firefighting equipment due to structural concerns at its current location on State Fair Boulevard.

Built in 1969, the Fire Department's Maintenance Division handles repair and maintenance for the city's fire trucks and support vehicles, as well as repairing firefighting equipment, inventory and supply distribution.

But structure concerns began in September 2024 when the city engineering department identified some small cracks in the foundation. It soon led to more issues exposed on July 18, 2024, when an apparatus fell through the floor, revealing more damage and causing inspectors to deem the floor unsafe for heavy loads.

If a new location cannot be found, a new storage location will have to be used instead.

Fire Chief Michael Monds reported that the next steps would be to move to another location for the next five years until the department could assess and fix the damage.

“A lot of things go on there besides mechanical work,” said Monds. “Getting these things fixed in a timely manner is important to make sure our spare apparatus, along with our frontline apparatus, isn't putting our firefighters or our constituents at risk.”

Mechanics are currently working outside to keep the trucks running, but Monds says that with cold weather incoming, finding a temporary indoor space to work has reached crisis level.

“We have a gun to our head because we're at a really tight time crunch to actually find a building to get into," said Monds. "Right now, Syracuse University is letting us use one of their garage bays up on Ainsley Drive. It doesn't fit our whole apparatus, so it's a really emergency type of situation."
