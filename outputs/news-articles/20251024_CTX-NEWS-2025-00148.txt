SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Syracuse Police and the Syracuse Department of Neighborhood & Business Development are alerting businesses about a scam involving fraudulent zoning invoices.

The police department said fake bills have been sent to multiple businesses across Syracuse over the past couple of months. The fake notices instruct businesses to pay for an approval fee, submission fee, environmental impact fee and final review fee via wire transfer.

A photo of the fake invoices requesting wire transfers. (Oct. 24, 2025) (PHOTO SOURCE: SYRACUSE POLICE DEPARTMENT)

Police said a key way to tell if the invoices are fraudulent is that the city itself would never ask a business to wire transfer money for a zoning invoice.

Anyone with questions about city zoning fees or who receives an invoice in the mail, please contact the City of Syracuse Division of Code Enforcement at 315-442-8695 to ensure the invoice is legitimate.
