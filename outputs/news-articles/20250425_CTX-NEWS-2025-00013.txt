Syracuse Mayor taking Nob Hill to Supreme Court

by Hannah Devanny

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Mayor Ben Walsh announced on Thursday that he will be taking management of Nob Hill Apartments to the state Supreme Court. This is due to countless complaints received, code violations and over $340,000 accrued in statutory fines. These violations are because of broken elevators, inoperable boilers, inadequate heat, no hot water, and other maintenance and repair issues.

Tenants at the apartment complex are frustrated with the conditions they've been forced to live in. Most of the ongoing complaints are being heard from residents living in Building 3.

Mayor Ben Walsh said, "We are not gonna tolerate slumlords."

This is after months of tenants complaining about a plethora of issues. They have been seeing emails and calling Mayfair Management, the owners of the complex. In return, receiving the same answer every time and nothing changing.

Fire alarms aren't working, extinguishers are out of date and there hasn't been any hot water for residents for over a month.

Soggy Sunday in CNY, spring warmth back to winter chill and snowflakes

Andre Chambers, a tenant for over 8 years, told CNY Central the water is so cold, a couple of weeks ago he had to take his children to a nearby hotel for a warm shower.

Another tenant said he bathes by boiling water over his stove, in order to have warm water.

Mayfair Management opened a couple of vacant apartments in a different building for tenants to shower there and have warm water. However, there's a schedule for showering. The apartments are only open from 9 a.m. to 9 p.m. Monday through Friday. The communal showers are also allegedly not cleaned.

All of this is causing tenants questioning whether or not they should have to pay their rent.
