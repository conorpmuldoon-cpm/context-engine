Residents worried about property tax increase due to $1.8 million plan

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Syracuse City Officials have a three-year project planned to reassess the value of residents' homes. This $1.8 million project is likely to increase evaluations by more than 70%.

Upon hearing the news, some residents felt very worried. Lynne Werbeck has lived in Tipperary Hill for years. Her property was assessed a couple of years ago, which raised her property taxes. She hopes this doesn't happen again.

"I'm having a hard time with the fact that they may increase again," Werbeck said.

If city councils receive approval for this project, it will start in the spring.

City officials met with Syracuse's Common Council on Wednesday to get the approval. However, the voting was delayed due to concerns the council had with the management and oversight of the project. $1.8 million plan is with G.A.R. Associates, a Buffalo property assessment company.

If this plan gets approved, more than 41,000 properties in the city will be reevaluated.

Jen Schultz, a resident of Sedgewick, isn't surprised about the potential new plans.

"I already know that New York State is one of the highest with property taxes in the country," Schultz said.

While she may not be surprised, she still expressed concern.

"With the cost of living today, inflation and everything that's going on in this crazy world right now, anytime prices go up, it's concerning," Schultz said.

The Sedgewick resident went on to say that if property taxes were to go up, she and her family would have to take another look at their budget. This is something that did not have to be done for the past 10 years.
