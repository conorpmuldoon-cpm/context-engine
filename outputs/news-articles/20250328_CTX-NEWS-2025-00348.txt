Former Sears Building to be site of state's first workforce innovation hub

by Matthew Benninger

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Governor Kathy Hochul announced the selection of the former Sears Building at 1300 South Salina St. to be the future ON-RAMP Workforce Innovation Hub. It will be the first of four in the state.

Governor Hochul said the hub will anchor a broader revitalization site, which is being led by the City of Syracuse and CenterState CEO. Hochul said the site will be a "gateway" for people looking for work as well as manufacturing and construction firms.

The One Network for Regional Advanced Manufacturing Partnerships, or ON-RAMP, program is a $200 million investment that seeks to connect skilled New Yorkers with careers in manufacturing industries.

Her office noted that Empire State Development's Board of Directors approved $8.5 million in funding for the flagship center, which is part of Governor Hochul’s broader $200 million ON-RAMP initiative.

Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh said the announcement is great for the city's south side community.

"In alignment with Syracuse Surge, our strategy for inclusive growth in the new economy, the creation of a larger workforce training hub will accelerate our efforts to uplift residents through long-term, sustainable employment and economic growth," said Walsh. "I look forward to continued neighborhood engagement as this project moves forward."

Staff for the center will be temporarily housed at CenterState CEO at 115 West Fayette St. while the South Salina Street site is being redeveloped. The Initial training programs will focus on career exploration, English for Speakers of Other Languages, financial empowerment and trade-specific skills for construction and manufacturing. There will also be training in construction and manufacturing and more.

Centers are also planned for the Capital Region, the Mohawk Valley and the Finger Lakes.
