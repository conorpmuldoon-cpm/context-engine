Syracuse issues 21,000 tickets for School Zone Traffic Enforcement violations in November

by Brandon Roth

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Syracuse City officials announced that they issued 21,000 tickets in November.

That number is the result of the first official month of the School Zone Traffic Enforcement Program. Over the summer, the city installed traffic cameras, primarily in zones a quarter-mile around schools, to monitor drivers' speeds.

Of the 21,000 tickets, most were violations about a driver's speed in school zones. Roughly 10% of the tickets were sent to people who drove through red lights.

The city said it reported 60% decrease in speeding violations compared to October. However, the city noted that it had fewer speed and red light cameras running in November compared to October. Additionally, there were fewer school days in November compared to October.

The program uses the cameras to capture the license plates of speeding vehicles. Anyone caught violating the speed limit or going through a red light in a school zone is mailed a ticket. Those tickets show the speed they were going, when and where the violation occurred. It also includes a picture of the driver's vehicle at the moment of the violation.

For the city's School Bus Stop Arm Program, 645 tickets were issued for violations in November. 361 tickets were issued in October.

The School Bus Stop Arm Program issues violations with cameras mounted on the sides of school buses. Drivers are considered in violation of the program if they pass a school bus with its stop arm out.
