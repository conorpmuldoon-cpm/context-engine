SYRACUSE, N.Y. — A project to transform the East Adams neighborhood in Syracuse's 15th Ward will take longer than initially planned.

The Syracuse Planning Commission has approved an 18-month extension for the project, which is managed by the Syracuse Housing Authority. The project is now slated for completion in September 2027.

MORE: Syracuse officials explore options as Trump kills funding for neighborhood rebuild

Leaders at the Housing Authority have faced scrutiny for their actions on this project and others in the city, with some neighbors saying changes need to be made to help better serve tenants.

The Syracuse Housing Authority explained that the extension request was necessary because the lender funding the project expressed concerns about meeting the original completion date, which was set for spring of next year.
