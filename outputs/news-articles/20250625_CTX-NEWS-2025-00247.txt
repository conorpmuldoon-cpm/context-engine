Syracuse, N.Y. — Mayor Ben Walsh said there will be more police around city pools in the coming weeks after a teen was fatally shot Tuesday outside a city pool in the Strathmore neighborhood.
A 15-year-old boy was shot in the abdomen by another person just outside the Upper Onondaga Park Pool’s fence around 2 p.m. He later died at Upstate University Hospital.
The shooter is not in custody, and the gun has not been found, Syracuse Police Chief Joseph Cecile said.
“Losing a teenager with so much life ahead makes this tragedy even more devastating,” Walsh said in a statement Tuesday. “As I was leaving the park this afternoon, it made me sad and angry to see an empty pool at a time it should have been filled with activity.”
The reopening date for the Upper Onondaga Park Pool - where the 15-year-old was killed - has not yet been decided.
The city’s Department of Parks, Recreation and Youth Programs is providing support to the staff members who were on duty at the time of the shooting, he said.
