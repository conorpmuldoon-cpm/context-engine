Syracuse parking garage closes due to structural concerns

Drivers heading into downtown Syracuse will need to find alternative parking options as the parking garage at 300 East Fayette Street is closing due to structural concerns.

An inspection by an engineering firm working with the city revealed significant signs of deterioration in the 41-year-old structure.

Jake DeShaw, the city's deputy commissioner of code enforcement, acknowledged the inconvenience caused by the closure. "We realize this is an inconvenience for everybody parked in the garage, including city staff. We have about 100-150 people who park here daily who work out of One Park Place or at City Hall, plus there's private monthly parkers as well. So we understand it's a big inconvenience," DeShaw said. He emphasized, "We're always going to put safety first."
