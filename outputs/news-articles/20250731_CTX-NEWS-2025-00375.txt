SYRACUSE, N.Y. (WSYR-TV) — Syracuse City Auditor Alexander Marion compiled an analysis of vacant structure fires in 2024 with his team to prevent more from occurring across city neighborhoods.

The report, titled “Following the Fire,” is over 100 pages long. The document dove into the city’s number of vacant properties, how many buildings were affected by the flames, the response after a fire, and what caused the structures to fall into poor conditions in the first place.

“The city of Syracuse had 87 structure fires in 2024, and 25 of them were in vacant buildings,” Marion said. “That is about a third of all of our fires happening in vacant buildings.”

Marion said these fires are not only dangerous to neighbors, but also to first responders.

“Anytime we can prevent a situation where our firefighters have to run into a burning building, we should be taking steps to prevent that from happening,” he said. “God forbid something worse happens in a vacant structure fire that could have been prevented.”

The report also details Marion’s recommendation for the city to take immediate action by identifying any patterns in vacant structures. This will allow them to stage early interventions and use, what he calls, “the FEMA standard of labeling those buildings.”

“24×24 signs with a giant red ‘X’ on them,” he said. “This is how people will know from a distance that a property is not safe and they should not enter.”

Marion also asked the city to be more aggressive with inspections of properties that have caught fire, to closely inspect the full portfolio of any property owner who has a unit declared “unfit,” and to inspect any property with a utility shut off.
