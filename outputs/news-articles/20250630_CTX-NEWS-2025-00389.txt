SYRACUSE, N.Y. (WSYR-TV) — It will be a new chapter for not just 250 students, but also the Syracuse City School District, when the doors to the STEAM High School open this September.
“We’re starting at ninth grade for this year coming up, and then we’ll ladder up, so then it will be ninth and tenth grade,” said Erin Noto, the Executive Director of Career and Technical Education for the school district.
The school offers programs like civil engineering, robotics, data analytics, and performing arts for students.
