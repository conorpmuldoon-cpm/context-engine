Syracuse council debates $900K payroll modernization investment with Mosaic Consulting

The Syracuse Common Council called a special meeting on Wednesday to have the Walsh administration explain why it needs another $900,000 for an outside consultant.

Counselors learned that the new software would also require nearly $750,000 each year to keep it running.

Five years after the Payroll Modernization Project began, the Finance Commissioner admitted that spending on outside consultants was too high.

"2023-24 illustrates that external spending got out of control," said Syracuse Finance Commissioner Mike Cannizzaro. “There are license fees associated with Kronos support. But now we're trying to do this on the cheap.”

During the summer of 2025, the city put out a request for proposals, choosing Mosaic Consulting Group to implement a new software system.

Mosaic is based in Nashville, Tennessee and specializes in the implementation of UKG software. The other two bidders were ATZ Solution LLC of Charlotte, NC and Consultadd Inc. out of New York City. No local bids were submitted.

“They seemed like the best partner for us, understood the challenges, limited resources for a city our size,” said Cannizzaro. “We would be working with the project team we met with, I know that was a problem with previous contractors, as far as outsourcing. “

The administration wants to spend on Mosaic, which is approved now, but admits the work will not start under the Walsh administration. Mayor Ben Walsh's term ends at the end of December.

It is not clear whether the Council will move forward to vote on the $900,000 spending budget soon.

More dialogue is likely to happen with the Walsh Administration before a vote is cast.
