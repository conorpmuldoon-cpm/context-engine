James Pass Arboretum to Close Aug. 11 and 12 for tree work

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The City of Syracuse Department of Parks & Recreation’s Forestry Division will temporarily close James Pass Arboretum on Monday, Aug. 11 and Tuesday, Aug. 12 for tree removal and maintenance work.

The closure is part of the ongoing Pass Arboretum Improvement Plan. To ensure public safety, all entrances to the park will be fenced off beginning at 7 a.m. on Aug. 11. The park is expected to reopen by approximately 4 p.m. on Aug. 12.

Forestry crews will remove trees that are invasive, dead, structurally unsound, or nearing the end of their lifespan. Other trees will be pruned to remove deadwood and improve their structure, health, and storm resilience.
