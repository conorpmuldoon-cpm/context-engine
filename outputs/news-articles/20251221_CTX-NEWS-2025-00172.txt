SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The Syracuse Police Department has reported a notable decrease in some crime rates as the year draws to a close. According to the department, both violent and property crimes have seen significant reductions, with violent crime down over 18% and property crime, including stolen vehicles and burglaries, down over 24%. "I think this year went well stat-wise," said said Chief of Police Joe Cecile. "We're not ready to throw our hands up and declare success," he continued.

You can find this year's series of COMPSTAT reports here. Screenshots of the report will be featured throughout this story.

Officials attribute this success to proactive policing efforts. "The cops have done a great job taking down crime," said the Chief.

One of the standout graphs included in this week's COMPSTAT report is one depicting the amount of car thefts this year. On the week of November 10th, there was a major spike. "It's an anomaly in the sense that we can't point to any specific thing. Whether its weather, whether its kids out of school, whether it is a new TikTok challenge," said the Chief.

A screenshot of the COMPSTAT report this week shows the number of car thefts since September.{ }

The department also highlighted a decrease in shootings with injuries and fatal shootings, crediting outreach workers and preventive measures for the improvement. In 2020, there were around 165, decreasing each year to 56 in 2025.
