SYRACUSE, N.Y. — A new pilot program in the city of Syracuse is decreasing the amount of motor vehicle accidents involving deer.

The city's deer management plan began using traps for the first time in 2025 to help capture the animals.

Between the traps and wildlife officers tracking and shooting the deer, the city was able to remove 137 white tailed deer this year.

The city says this program has led to less car accidents, instances of Lyme disease and damage to parks and private property since its start.
