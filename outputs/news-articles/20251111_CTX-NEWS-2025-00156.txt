SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The Syracuse Common Council has approved funding for the Exalt Youth program, an initiative aimed at providing an alternative to incarceration for the city's youth.

The program seeks to inspire and support young people involved with the criminal justice system by connecting them with internships and professional opportunities.

Syracuse Common Councilor Chol Majok expressed optimism about the program's impact, saying, "My hope is that Exalt coming into the city of Syracuse will make a difference in lives, in the lives of the citizens of our city, and there will be the difference that we are hoping for."
