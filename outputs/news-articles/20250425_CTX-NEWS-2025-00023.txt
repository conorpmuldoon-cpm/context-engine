SYRACUSE, N.Y. — At 6 p.m. on Friday night, the Syracuse Housing Authority will hold a required public hearing on its draft five-year annual plan at its headquarters at 516 Burt St.

The plan provides a "ready source for interested parties," regarding its operations, programs and services. The I-Team found the public hearing notice in a different section of the SHA website than where public meeting notices usually show up.

The 49-page plan outlines basic information about SHA, including 2,157 public housing units as of July, when its fiscal year begins. It also shows the $62 million in funding they expect to receive for housing people and continuing to improve the quality of existing housing stock, per the report.

The need is great, with nearly 10,000 families on Syracuse's public housing waiting list as of March 7. More than 80% are considered "extremely low income." That works out to 8,255 families.

The still-paused Children Rising Center is also included in the draft plan document. Construction was supposed to start on the $32 million center this month, but stakeholders said the Syracuse Housing Authority missed deadlines to help them secure the rest of the funding needed for the project. SHA and its Executive Director, Bill Simmons, continue to deny that any deadlines were missed.

The I-Team previously uncovered plans moving forward with a brand new central office for the Housing Authority, and those plans are also in the document. It reads, "SHA has been working on relocating to a new central office building, has entered into a contract with a company, and plans for a new central office building are progressing."
