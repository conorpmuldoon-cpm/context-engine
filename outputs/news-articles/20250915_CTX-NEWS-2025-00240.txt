Syracuse, N.Y. – Onondaga County Executive Ryan McMahon today is proposing a $1.6 billion budget for 2026 that, among other initiatives, would include $6 million for a new sheriff’s helicopter and $700,000 to begin using drones to respond to 911 calls.
McMahon presented his proposal to the county Legislature at 11 a.m. today.
His budget would increase overall spending by 3% but would not raise the amount of money collected in property taxes, which would remain flat at $146 million.
Thanks to rising property values, county officials can lower the tax rate without reducing the overall levy because assessments have risen. McMahon proposes to decrease the tax rate 11% to $2.93 per $1,000 of assessed value, which would save the owner of a $300,000 house $114.
County residents will give back some of that savings in the form of higher sewer rates. The proposed sewer charge for a household would increase $25 — or 5.5% — to $478.58 a year.
Sales tax collections are projected to increase 1.5% next year.
McMahon’s budget proposal includes renewed funding for several ongoing priorities. He devotes $1.23 million to fight lead poisoning, on top of roughly $12 million allocated in previous budgets. He would provide $2.3 million to the Early Childhood Alliance. And he plans to continue funding
mental health services
in local schools.
Among major new initiatives is McMahon’s plan to spend roughly $6 million to replace Air One, the helicopter operated by the sheriff’s office. The 25-year-old helicopter is outdated, McMahon says, and should be replaced by a state-of-the-art unit. Air One is used to respond to major criminal incidents, for search and rescue operations, and other needs.
McMahon also proposes $700,000 to launch a drone program to respond to emergencies. The “drone as a first responder” program could provide information to police or firefighters before they arrive at the scene, or drones could be sent into situations that are too dangerous for a human responder, McMahon says.
The city of Syracuse is considering a
similar initiative to use drones
to respond to some police calls in the city. The Common Council will hold a public hearing at 5:30 p.m. tonight to hear residents’ opinions on the plan.
McMahon proposes spending about $300,000 to outfit every sheriff’s deputy at the Justice Center jail and Jamesville prison with body-worn cameras. Road patrol deputies have worn cameras for several years, and cameras for custody deputies are long overdue, McMahon says.
McMahon’s budget proposal will be reviewed by county lawmakers beginning later this week. The first session will be at 9:30 a.m. Wednesday, when county officials will present an overall introduction to the budget and the capital spending plan.
