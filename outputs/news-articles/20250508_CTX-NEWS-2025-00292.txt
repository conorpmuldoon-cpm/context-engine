Syracuse, N.Y. — In a meeting packed with about 100 concerned city employees, the Syracuse Common Council unanimously — and without any discussion — passed more than
80 budget amendments
that cut proposed spending by about $16 million.
It was the final step in a budget review process that Mayor Ben Walsh said was “unprecedented” in its secrecy, and one that he and his senior staff are taking as a personal affront. The cuts that council approved, he said, are “draconian and dangerous.”
With about a dozen senior staff standing behind him, the mayor was visibly angry during an hour-long press conference following the council’s vote. Walsh said he and his team had reached out multiple times to councilors attempting to engage in budget negotiations, but they first learned of the cuts when syracuse.com
posted a story
Wednesday afternoon. A meeting agenda with the listed budget amendments did not go out until 11 p.m.
“The term unprecedented has been used to describe this budgeting process,” Walsh said, referring to Councilor Pat Hogan’s description of what lawmakers felt they had to do with the budget. “What is unprecedented is the secrecy and lack of transparency exhibited by the council throughout this process.”
Hogan read a statement ahead of Thursday’s voting, which was the only comment any councilor made during the meeting.
“The Common Council’s budget revisions have been made with care,” he said. “Notably, they do not include a property tax increase, no water fee increase and there are no reductions to police or fire department staffing, and no city jobs will be lost as a result of these changes. ... As stewards of the City and its taxpayers, the Common Council remains committed to ensuring a stable financial future for the City of Syracuse.”
