City Officials comment on the progress of I-81 Viaduct Project

by Hannah Devanny

Tue, September 16, 2025 at 11:29 PM

The progress of the I-81 Viaduct Project has become noticeably apparent to drivers in and around the city of Syracuse. According to the Project Director for the city of Syracuse, Joe Driscoll, the reasoning behind the project is to make the city more comfortable and accessible for people walking and biking.

City Officials said the response from the public about the project has been mixed so far.

Driscoll told CNY Central that the city has heard a lot of positive feedback from Syracuse residents about the project. In particular, the speed at which they're seeing updates to construction.

"People will drive by on Monday and see there's no bridge there," said Driscoll. "Then, by Thursday or Friday, there's already a skeleton of a bridge."

However, the constant construction also comes with negative feedback.

City of Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh said, "People are frustrated with the traffic."

Another issue City Leaders have heard from community members is about the communication.

"To really reach every home and every person is just a real challenge to get information to everybody," said Driscoll.

Driscoll said the progress we've seen so far is only the beginning. More construction and updates are on the way.
