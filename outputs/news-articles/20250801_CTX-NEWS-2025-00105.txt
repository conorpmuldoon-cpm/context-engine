Box truck strikes Parkway Bridge again

SALINA, N.Y. — A box truck has struck the underside of the Parkway Bridge in Salina again.

The strike occurred around 1:30 p.m. on Friday. The Onondaga County Sheriff's Office responded to the scene. 511NY reported that all lanes of traffic are closed in the area from Oswego Street to Park Street.

The timing of the strike comes just two days after Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh suggested using "clanker" devices to prevent bridge strikes underneath the Park Street railroad bridge in his city. It's another common spot where large trucks get stuck after hitting the bottom of the structure.

The clanker devices are set up ahead of the bridge. If a truck strikes them, they make a loud noise, alerting the driver of the truck that they will not clear the upcoming bridge.
