SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The Syracuse Planning Commission discussed and approved the master site plan for the former Lafayette Country Club on Monday, August 18.

It was the first of what will be a series of hurdles, that developers need to clear before they can begin constructing a 263-unit housing development on the former country club property.

A major site review, different environmental impact studies and specific code enforcements will need to be approved before the project gets a full go-ahead from the city of Syracuse.

According to developers, the homes will range from $350,000 to $1 million and executive homes will also be available for millions of dollars.

The development also includes resubdivision of multiple new lots for each single-family house, and establishment of seven new public roads to accommodate the additional demand for transportation purposes.
