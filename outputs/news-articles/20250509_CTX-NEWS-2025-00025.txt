SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The democratic primary race will play a pivotal role in who becomes the next mayor.

Democrats outnumber Republicans in the city, and that means the winner of the primary in June is likely to be the next mayor.

Deputy Mayor Sharon Owens railed against the budget cuts today.

Councilors Pat Hogan and Chol Majok championed them.

Sharon Owens spoke on the podium at the press conference, and she was extremely frustrated. She said that not one of the council members gave a call to the city.

“This is the most egregious abuse of power I’ve ever seen,” said Deputy Mayor Sharon Owens.

Owens said herself and the cities department were blindsided by the common council’s decision to amend the budget.

“We got the information late last night but you couldn’t give these folks the respect enough to call them to say we’re entertaining $3.5 million out of your budget, how will that impact you,” said Owens.

Deputy mayor Owens is one of three democratic candidates who are running for mayor. The other two are Chol Majok and Pat Hogan, and they are on the same Common Council that rejected the budget.

“I don’t know what’s behind this. I don’t know what is behind the process that we just went through so I don’t know, it’s for you and the taxpayers to answer that question,” said Owens

Common Councilors Chol Majok and Pat Hogan said this decision to amend the budget was not for political reasons but for fiscal reasons after the city’s budget has grown each year.

“Our city can no longer operate like this. In the next 8 years it’s going to be another hundred million or more, our taxpayers just can’t maintain that.”

Common councilor Pat Hogan said, “We decided because our fiduciary responsibility to cut the budget in order to preserve the integrity of the city because frankly his budget spent way too much money.”

CNY Central asked Majok and Hogan if this wasn’t for political reasons then why does the city claim say they knew nothing about this?

“It was submitted last night for the vote today and we were trying to crunch our numbers, and we needed time and that’s the best time we could have submitted it,” said Majok.
