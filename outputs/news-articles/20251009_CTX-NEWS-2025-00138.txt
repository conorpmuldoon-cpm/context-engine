Syracuse City Schools are preparing for a leadership transition as Superintendent Anthony Davis attended his final board meeting.

Davis, who has led the district since 2022, will step down at the end of the month. The meeting was marked by recognition and gratitude from local and federal leaders.

Mayor Ben Walsh praised Davis, saying, "You have a heart for our children and for this district and you have given your career to young people in our community."

Rep. John Mannion announced a House of Representatives proclamation to honor Davis's retirement on Oct. 31.

Davis expressed appreciation for the recognition, stating, "It means I've developed some great relationships. It means people actually see what I was trying to model."

Syracuse schools bid farewell to superintendent Anthony Davis amid challenges

The celebration comes amid district controversies, including payroll issues and below-average proficiency scores in subjects like ELA and math. The district is considering hiring Davis as a consultant for up to $125,000 to provide coaching to faculty.

Deputy Superintendent Pamela Odom is set to succeed Davis. Davis expressed confidence in her abilities, saying, "Pamela Odom is the right person... by her working with me daily as a deputy superintendent, she has the knowledge, she's seen the job up close."
