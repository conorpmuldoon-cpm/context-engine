One police officer to face discipline following viral forceful arrest in Syracuse

by Nicole La Fiandra

Fri, April 18, 2025 at 10:58 AM
Updated Fri, April 18, 2025 at 6:05 PM
Syracuse Police Chief Joseph Cecile said an officer will face discipline following an arrest on South Avenue on April 9, 2025.

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Syracuse Chief of Police Joseph Cecile confirmed on Friday that one officer will be disciplined following an April 9 arrest that was captured by phone video. That video garnered thousands of views online.

PREVIOUS REPORTING | Syracuse Police Chief stops short of detailing "discipline" following viral arrest video, Police Chief: Officers will face discipline in viral South Avenue arrests

In the video that went viral on Facebook, two men are seen being forcefully arrested by officers on South Avenue. They were initially stopped by police for having tinted windows on their vehicle. In the video, officers can be seen taking down the men before putting them into handcuffs.

One of the officers is seen striking one of the men in the head. Cecile defended the hits and referred to them as "controlled strikes." The chief said it follows the Police Department's use-of-force policy to get people to put their hands over their heads.

One of the men being arrested in the video, Jaumar Chandler, 21, said he was traumatized by the ordeal.
