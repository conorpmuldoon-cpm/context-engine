Syracuse Police drone program green-lit amid privacy concerns

by Nicole La Fiandra

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The Syracuse Police Department's Drone First Response Program is moving forward with a one-year trial, despite concerns over privacy.

The program, green-lit by the Common Council, will be evaluated after a year to determine its effectiveness.

PREVIOUS REPORTING | Syracuse seeking public comment on proposed 'surveillance technology' for police use

Councilor Chol Majok emphasized the importance of reviewing first-year data before making a long-term commitment and Councilor Corey Williams said once the technology is implemented, it will be tough to put the "genie back in the bottle."

"I truly don't envision a scenario in which a year from now, where we pull the technology away and that is my concern at this point in time when looking out at the future," Williams said.

Constituents have raised privacy concerns, but officers have compared the drone policy to that of body cameras.

"We're not going to use this at all to invade their privacy," officers said.

First Deputy Chief Richard Shoff assured the public, stating, "We're not going to do random surveillance if we responded to a specific complaint. Second, it's ingrained in all of our policies. Right, we can't target anyone based on enumerated characteristics."

Shoff added, "We're not going to use the drones for that kind of surveillance. 'See what's going on, hey, is there anything here or there?' It's all going to be responding to calls."

The drones will not have audio capabilities and a website will be available to show where the drones are being deployed.
