SYRACUSE, N.Y. — City of Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh's proposed $348 million spending plan for FY-2025-2026 is under scrutiny by the Common Council.

They fired back with 80 amendments and proposed cuts last week. The Mayor told the I-team he'll be going through each amendment carefully before announcing his vetoes.

PREVIOUS COVERAGE | Independent firm, used by Walsh Administration, finds 26 'red flags' with mayor's budget

He has until Monday to do that, and told CNY Central he intends to take until then to present his vetoes publicly.

The I-Team took a look back through his budgets over his tenure leading Syracuse. City Auditor Alex Marion's office is tracking the Mayor's proposed budget.

"The largest driver of our budgets is salaries," said Marion. He added, "Many of those have gone up because of labor contracts that result in higher wages paid to employees over the years, and annual raises."

He said, "Many of the increases we saw in this year's budget are largely out of the city's control."

Marion told the I-Team there's been a steady increase in funding for public safety and the Department of Public Works over Mayor Walsh's tenure.
