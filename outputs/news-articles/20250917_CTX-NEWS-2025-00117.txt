SYRACUSE, N.Y. — A new mural is taking shape on Syracuse's Southside at the intersection of Taylor and State Street, with city leaders expressing hopes that the artwork will not only beautify the area but also enhance safety.

"Once the viaduct comes down we want to make sure that this neighborhood, the Southside, has multimodal connectivity so it’s safer for pedestrians, safer for people on bikes, Joe Driscoll, the I-81 Project Director for the City of Syracuse, said. "We are studying all these corridors but as a part of that we wanted to illustrate to the community what can change with a little bit of paint and a little inspiration, so we have."

The mural, an effort by a large team of volunteers, is intended to bring a vibrant splash of color to the city while promoting safer streets. The project is designed to last for one year, with city crews committed to maintaining the artwork.

"This is a one-year installation. We will be coming back through every month, every couple of weeks just to check it and make sure everything is up to date. So me and the city staff will keep an eye on it and make sure it is up to date," Driscoll said.
