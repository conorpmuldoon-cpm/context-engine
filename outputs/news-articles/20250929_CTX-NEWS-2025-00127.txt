SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The Syracuse Police Department revealed what prompted city hall and the surrounding streets to be evacuated and shut down on Monday afternoon.

The police department said the "suspicious package" was actually a New York State Department of Transportation listening device that is used to monitor noise from I-81. Police said that the device was placed there by a contractor on Sept. 25.

The listening device, which was a Pelican box with a microphone, was strapped to a tree just outside the city hall. It was reported to the Syracuse Fire Department by a passerby.

Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh said his administration and the State DOT are in contact about the devices and their use in the city. He said it remains under investigation as to how that device ended up in a tree. Typically, they are tied to utility poles, said Walsh, although he admitted he's "no expert" on the subject.

He said normally the DOT and the city are in communication about where and when the devices are placed.

Syracuse Police reveal 'suspicious package' that evacuated city hall

"This one did catch us by surprise," Walsh said. "The good news is that somebody saw something and they said something."

The evacuation of city hall occurred just before noon. Dispatch recordings revealed an officer identifying the package as "suspicious" and that the area of Montgomery and Washington Streets would need to be shut down. A police drone was seen examining the box.

The listening device remains up as of Monday afternoon. Walsh said it's expected to come soon.
