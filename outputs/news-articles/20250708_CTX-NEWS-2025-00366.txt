Community activist talks about rash of violent crime, young suspects in Syracuse

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Clifford Ryan is behind the nonprofit community group OG's Against Violence in Syracuse. He lost one of his sons at 17 to gun violence in 1999.

For years now, he's been holding up his signs in areas where violent crimes happen. CNY Central went to Ryan following a homicide near the Onondaga Park pool, and a crash involving a stolen vehicle in the past couple of weeks.

In both cases, teens came out as suspects identified by law enforcement.

Data from the New York State Division of Criminal Justice Services shows that between 2019 and 2023, more than 4,000 instances where there was police contact with individuals younger than 18, either formal arrests or police contact with youth where there is probable cause that a crime was committed but the agency did not file formal charges.

CNY Central met Ryan near Butternut and Lodi Streets in Syracuse.

"Understanding what you're facing and what you're dealing with, and continuing to try to do something to be a deterrent in the community as far as it's concerned, is very, very important. That's the mission that you see me on," said Ryan.

Retired NYS Police Commander Frank Coots said there's no single thing to point to when it comes to youth violence.
