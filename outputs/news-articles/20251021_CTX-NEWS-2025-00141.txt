SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Chicago-based Clear Investment Group allowed CNY Central to enter the troubled Skyline Apartments.

Clear has pledged to transform the horrific building on James Street in Syracuse into a habitable space for city residents. In the past, the building was plagued by a murder, drug activity, vermin, a fire, burst water pipes and innumerable other issues.

Yet, CNY Central cameras picked up progress on the building that's expected to be ready for residents by April 30, 2026. By then, it'll be known as "The Metropolitan." The group also let cameras into rooms that still needed to be repaired.

Managing Director & Chief Financial Officer Razi Uddin said Clear has modernized the building's elevators, upgraded its fire alarm and security systems and completed finishes on the first three floors of the building.

He noted that Clear Investment Group wants to move some residents into the building by Dec. 31.

"Everything is lining up," said Uddin. "The stars are aligning. The planets are aligning for us to be at partial access."

Skyline Apartments has been prone to flooding from burst pipes. It's one of the more infamous issues that the building had endured. Uddin noted that many floors and boilers needed to be replaced due to flooding. He noted that the building's mechanical room had taken in 11 feet of water.

He said fixing issues related to flooding was a big undertaking.

"The reality is that you know it wasn't really a fault of our own," said Uddin. "But unfortunately, when you have problems that occur on projects like this, you figure it out and you move forward. I mean, this isn't the first project we've done and we have multiple projects going on around the country and this is what we do."

Clear took over the property from Green Skyline Apartments, LLC in 2023 and stated that the problems it "inherited" were Green's fault.

On Sept. 22, Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh announced that the city reached an agreement that commits Clear to fix the property for future tenants after taking the company to court. At the time, the city threatened legal action against Clear, claiming that the company was dragging its feet on repairs and letting the building fall into further disrepair.

Clear Investment Group CEO Amy Rubenstein said she was baffled by the lawsuit.

"Our priority has always been to create safe, respectable communities for our residents,” Rubenstein told CNY Central in April. “While we did not cause the problems that led to this settlement, we accept the responsibility of addressing them. We are committed to working collaboratively to ensure Metropolitan is a place residents are proud to call home.”
