SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The City of Syracuse has exceeded its greenhouse gas emission reduction goals set in 2010 based on a 2024 Greenhouse Gas Emission Inventory report, the mayor's office announced on Thursday.

The initial targets aimed for a 40% reduction in emissions for city operations and a 7% reduction for the community by 2020. However, a recent report reveals that reductions reached as high as 67% for city operations and 29% for the community.

“The Syracuse Community should be proud of these results and the collective work that went into achieving these unprecedented measures,” said Mayor Walsh in a news release. “These reductions stem from years of private and public investments in climate resiliency. With this updated GHGI report, we can build on these findings with new and existing partners to make Syracuse a more sustainable and prosperous community for all.”

The city attributes this success to several initiatives, including the conversion to LED street and traffic lights, the use of fuel-efficient vehicles, and the optimization of waste collection routes.

The mayor's office said the next step for Syracuse will be to include the creation of a data-informed Climate Action Plan to promote the pursuit of net-zero emissions and climate resiliency within city limits.
