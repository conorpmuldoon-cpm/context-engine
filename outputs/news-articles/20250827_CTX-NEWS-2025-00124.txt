SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Tenants living in the Nob Hill apartment complex in Syracuse are continuing to voice their frustrations over ongoing issues with their living conditions, as a lawsuit against the building's owners remains unresolved.

The city initiated legal action in April over code violations, but the hearing has been postponed five times.

Neighbors have reported a range of problems, including lack of heat, unaddressed maintenance issues, and inadequate communication from management. One tenant, Tieasha Muhammad, described her experience.

"The counters were there was a fire in the kitchen," said Muhammed. "So, it was still the burnt counter still in there. And the sink wasn’t even hooked up."

Another tenant, Jackie Patterson, expressed frustration over long-standing issues, stating, "I’ve been on the waiting list for a new garage for two years."

Nob Hill tenants demand action amid ongoing legal battle with city

Kona Mahu, a former tenant, recounted, "We went without heat for two months, and they gave us a $600 credit."

The tenants have organized to demand action this year, with some considering a rent strike if conditions do not improve.

"All your voices together are one voice - and it’s loud. Me, by myself, is just a whisper," said Muhammad.
