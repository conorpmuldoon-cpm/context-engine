Syracuse, N.Y. — Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh appreciated the hundreds of submissions from the public for the city’s latest
snowplow-naming contest
, but one in particular held a special place in his heart.
As a big fan of the 1993 baseball film “The Sandlot,” Walsh loved the suggestion of “You’re Killing Me, Squalls.” The pun from that movie’s
iconic line
was among three winners Walsh unveiled today at a press conference by the city’s salt storage barn on Canal Street.
The other winners were “Syracuse Saltshaker” and “Snow Big Deal.” They emerged from more than 700 submissions made when the city announced the contest in July, a volume the mayor described as a “testament to how much passion we have for our snow.”
Syracuse also held a snowplow naming contest in 2020, when the winners were “Blizzard Beater,” “Salt City Express” and “Below Zero Hero.” Comedian John Oliver blasted the city for not being more creative
in a podcst.
Walsh noted that submissions with puns were especially appreciated in this year’s contest.
