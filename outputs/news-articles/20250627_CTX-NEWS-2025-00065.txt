State Supreme Court files injunction against illegal Syracuse weed shop

by Tyler Oldano

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh announced the New York State Supreme Court has filed an injunction against an illegal cannabis shop located at 916 Salina St.

The injunction stops The Herbal Center from selling or using cannabis. Walsh said the “The Herbal Center” presented itself as a private club.

“Operations like The Herbal Center are dangerous to public health and safety, undermine the state’s efforts to provide residents with safe recreational cannabis and hurt legitimate businesses that are following the rules,” said Mayor Walsh in a news release. “This case demonstrates the city‘s determination to protect our neighborhoods from businesses that operate illegally.”

As part of the ruling, the court rejected The Herbal Center's claim the city's cannabis law is unconstitutional and that the city should not have authorized inspections inside the illegal cannabis store. The judge ruled that The Herbal Center cannot expect privacy as it was operating as an illegal business.
