SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The second phase of the East Adams neighborhood transformation has begun.

The Syracuse Housing Authority and McCormack Baron Salazar, a developer and property manager, announced the beginning of the second phase in the city's 15th ward on Monday.

This phase begins remediation work at the corner of Burt and State Streets. The site is considered a "brownfield," meaning that it's potentially contaminated from a previous development. McCormack Baron Salazar said the site contains petroleum-impacted soils and elevated levels of lead and PAHs.

Remediation work includes:

Soil excavation and off-site disposal
Installation of a site-wide vapor barrier and sub-slab depressurization system
Import and placement of clean fill
Environmental monitoring throughout the process
Soggy Sunday in CNY, spring warmth back to winter chill and snowflakes

The transformation project seeks to construct 125 new affordable senior housing units as part of the Choice Neighborhoods Initiative. The 15th ward is one of the country's poorest zip codes.

The second phase is expected to go through the winter. Vertical construction is expected to begin in early 2026.
