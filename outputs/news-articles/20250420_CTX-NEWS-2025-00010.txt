SYRACUSE, N.Y. — On April 9th, Syracuse Police Officers forcefully arrested two men for their tinted windows. The video went viral on social media, garnering thousands of views. Chief of Police Joseph Cecile, now answering questions outside of a statement the department sent out after the incident first happened.

PREVIOUS REPORTING | Video of violent arrests by Syracuse Police officers generating community anger

The Chief says the department's use of force policy is publicly on the City of Syracuse's website. Click here to see it. He says the policy has been changed, updated, and amended over the years. Ultimately, everything is approved by the DCJS (Department of Criminal Justice).

"There are so many things in there about reasonableness, de-escalation, tasers, almost everything you could imagine, including those tactical strikes to the head and face." What the Chief is referring to here is something the surrounding community has closely watched and criticized online; short, controlled punches to the face.

It [the arrest] was well within our standards and procedures, as well as those provided by DCJS.

The Chief continued to say the current policy works to minimize injury to all parties involved, and those short punches serve a purpose, "Typically when someone is under arrest, their hands will go to their waistband if they have weapons, we do a tactical strike to the face or head, they then raise their hands over their head in defense," said the Chief. "It allows us to grab their wrists and put their hands behind their back."

He continued to say you can tell the difference between a tactical strike and a punitive strike. An officer will not "wind up" their arm to strike a person if they are attempting to get someone to raise their hands free of weapons. "They're very short range," said the Chief.

Chief Cecile confirmed on Friday one officer will be disciplined after the incident. "We found some minor violations on the body cam," he said. Ultimately, that officer will be retrained and sent back on the road to do their job.
