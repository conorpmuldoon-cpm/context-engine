SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The Syracuse codes department is asking for residents to speak up if they notice problems with their home.

Today our team got to visit with the codes department to learn more about their process during the annual "Healthy Housing 101" event

City code inspectors lead us through a vacant home discussing common health and safety issues when living in a house or a rental.

The inspectors mentioned common issues like lead, heat, water, smoke detectors and more. Codes officials say renters shouldn't be afraid to contact them when issues arise.

Matt Moran, a Syracuse City Code Enforcement Officer, emphasized the importance of communication between renters and code officials. "If you don't call in or we don't know about it, it won't get fixed. Plain and simple. Let the codes be the ones to take the shot and we'll fight for you."
