Parents feel extra safe with the new school zone spedometers

by Hannah Devanny

Mon, September 1, 2025 at 11:09 PM
Updated Mon, September 1, 2025 at 11:20 PM
Speedometers have been installed in some school zones in Syracuse. This one on James St. tracks how fast drivers are going, with a warning message for those going over 40 MPH. (PHOTO BY CNY CENTRAL).

SYRACUSE, N.Y. — The school year is upon us, and there will be more drivers on the roads. With more drivers comes more traffic and traffic concerns. In Syracuse, signs are up to warn drivers in real time about how fast they are going in school zones.

CNY Central tracked how fast cars were going in front of one of the speedometers on James Street. This was to see how many of the 50 cars we saw went faster than the 25-mile-an-hour speed limit. We found that 60% of cars were going above that speed limit.

The speedometer tracks up to about 38 miles an hour. After that, it will flash a warning, 'slow down'.

Parents in the area find that these new safety school zone tools are very helpful and important.

One parent, William Bennett, sees the school zone speedometers from a personal perspective.

Ten years ago, his daughter was walking with a friend to school one morning when something tragic happened. A truck hit both of them.

"My daughter had to learn how to walk again," said Bennett. "She was in a coma. Her friend died."

From then on, he has taught his children to be extra careful on the way to and from school. When Bennett is in a school zone, he takes safe driving very seriously. He goes as far as to drive under the speed limit.

Starting Wednesday, the speedometers have a 60-day grace period. After that, drivers can be ticketed $50 per traffic violation.
