SYRACUSE, N.Y. — There is new progress on plans to revitalize housing and build a community center for children in Syracuse.

The Syracuse Common Council voted on Monday, August 12, to move ahead with plans for several purchases with the Syracuse Housing Authority.

The approval by the Common Council creates a swap of Eastwood Heights and Latimer Terrace. The Syracuse Housing Authority is now back in charge of the Eastwood Heights project.

Latimer Terrace, the public housing block which the city would own through this deal, is the intended location for the now-paused Children Rising Center. As part of the early process for the federal Choice Neighborhoods Implementation grant, the city submitted a plan that meets the requirements for the neighborhood component last month.

That plan is now submitted to HUD and includes the intention to build the Children Rising Center by 2033, city officials said.
