The Syracuse Police Department Animal Control Unit is alerting the community to a recent increase in cases of Parvovirus (Parvo) among dogs in the Syracuse area.

A Facebook post was shared on Tuesday, July 22 on the Syracuse Police Department's page with the latest information on the cases and tips for prevention.

Parvo is a highly contagious and potentially deadly virus that primarily affects puppies and unvaccinated dogs. It can spread quickly through contact with infected animals or contaminated environments, according to the Facebook post.

Parvo can live on surfaces for years.

Syracuse Police asked neighbors to make sure their pets are up to date on their vaccinations and avoid allowing their dog to interact with unfamiliar or unvaccinated dogs.

If you believe your dog may have been exposed or is showing symptoms, contact your veterinarian immediately.

The symptoms commonly associated with Parvo include:

Fever
Weight Loss
Vomiting
Lethargy
Loss of Appetite
Severe or bloody diarrhea
