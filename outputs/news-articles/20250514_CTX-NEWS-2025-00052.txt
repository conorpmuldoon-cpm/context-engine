I-Team: Meeting between Mayor, Council President Pro Temp, others on Syracuse city budget

Syracuse, N.Y. — The I-Team learned that Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh, Finance Commissioner Michael Cannizzaro, Common Council President Pro Temp Pat Hogan and Common Councilor and Finance Committee Chair Corey Williams met Wednesday afternoon to talk about the city budget.

City Chief Policy Officer Greg Loh told CNY Central in a statement in part, "They met for about 45 minutes and shared their perspectives. It’s unclear what will happen as a result of the discussion or if future meetings will occur. Mayor Walsh made it clear he is open to compromise and further dialogue with Councilors."

President Pro Temp Hogan confirmed to the I-Team that it was Mayor Walsh who reached out to him requesting the meeting.

This comes as the mayor promised to issue vetoes to the 80 amendments the Common Council made to his original proposed $348 million budget. The Common Council sought to cut costs by $16 million by reducing spending across all city departments by 7%.

The Common Council's decision was based on an analysis of The Bonadio Group, an independent firm that is also used by the mayor's administration. The Common Council voted unanimously on May 8 to reject the budget.
