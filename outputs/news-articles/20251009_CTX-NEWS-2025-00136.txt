SYRACUSE, N.Y. — As Mayor Ben Walsh's term nears its end, the fate of the Christopher Columbus statue in downtown Syracuse remains unresolved.

Despite announcing plans to relocate the statue to Heritage Park in 2020, the monument still stands due to ongoing court battles and public debate over its racial implications.

Mayor Walsh confirmed that while the plan to move the statue is still in place, there is no set timeline for its relocation.

"It will be up through my term as mayor. I am still hopeful we can continue that process forward. But we are not going to rush it," Walsh said. He emphasized the importance of proceeding carefully, citing issues faced by other communities that rushed similar processes.

The New York Court of Appeals has allowed Syracuse to remove the statue, but litigation by the Columbus Monument Corporation has hindered progress.

"Because of the litigation involved we were really limited in our ability to move it forward," Walsh explained. "That process is ongoing so I can’t give you a timeline now."

As Walsh's term concludes in January, mayoral candidates have differing views on the statue's future.

The city's mayoral candidates have their own ideas for statue.

Alfonso Davis considers the removal a waste of resources, while Timonthy Rudd insists, "Columbus will stand."

Tom Babalon, the Republican candidate, highlighted the monument's original intent to combat prejudice against Italian Americans.

Deputy Mayor Sharon Owens, the Democratic candidate, supports the relocation process.

"We will follow through in the process we are in now to take a look at the heritage park process, to make a decision," said Owens. "In 2026 that will be a priority for me."
