To the Editor:
I am outraged by the recent vote by House Republicans to rescind the $30 million grant crucial for revitalizing Syracuse’s 15th Ward after the dismantling of the Interstate 81 viaduct (
“House GOP moves to cancel funding to revitalize poor neighborhood near I-81 in Syracuse,”
May 6, 2025).
This isn’t just a budget line item — it’s the lifeblood of a community long devastated by the scars of past infrastructure decisions, a community that has waited far too long for justice and renewal.
The 15th Ward was once a thriving, vibrant neighborhood before it was ruthlessly cleaved by I-81, displacing families and shattering the fabric of the community. This grant represented more than concrete and parks — it stood for hope, for economic opportunity, for healing historical wounds. Taking it back is not just fiscally shortsighted; it’s morally indefensible.
How can we, in good conscience, strip funding from a community that has already paid the price for the mistakes of the past? This is not about reducing the deficit — it’s about robbing underserved neighborhoods of the chance to rebuild. Children will lose safe places to play, families will lose the promise of affordable housing, and an entire community will lose faith in a government that promised to right these wrongs.
I implore our leaders to fight with everything they have to restore this funding. This is a battle for equity, for justice, and for the future of Syracuse. We cannot let political posturing crush the dreams of an entire neighborhood. Stand up. Speak out. Demand better.
George A. Wojtowycz
Syracuse
