SYRACUSE, N.Y. — Syracuse Housing Authority Executive Director Bill Simmons told the I-Team's Mary Kielar that he will not resign even in the face of disasters within his agency.

CNY Central arrived at a regular scheduled public meeting at 11:30 a.m. on Thursday, which is 30 minutes ahead of when it was scheduled to begin. However, the I-Team's Mary Kielar and a CNY Central Photojournalist was left to wait in the lobby with two community members and Syracuse City Hall's Commissioner of Neighborhood & Business Development.

40 minutes after the meeting was scheduled to start, CNY Central was finally allowed into the board room for the meeting, which had been going on in secret. The virtual participants' screens revealed that the meeting had been going on for more than an hour. The discussion was already on its third agenda item.

The secret meeting violated the New York State Open Meetings Law. Simmons admitted that votes to approve the past meeting minutes were taken and an executive session was called, therefore, the meeting was open to the public.
