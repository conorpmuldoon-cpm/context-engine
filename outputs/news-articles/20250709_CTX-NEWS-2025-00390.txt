SYRACUSE, N.Y. (WSYR-TV) — Syracuse’s affordable high-speed internet provider, Surge Link, expanded its coverage to more than 14,000 people on Tuesday, July 8.
In a press conference, Syracuse Mayor Ben Walsh announced the expansion, following the provider’s initial coverage of just over one thousand people.
Surge Link offers 100 Mbps of high-speed internet for $14.99, and the expansion gives more people who qualify a more affordable option. People who receive or qualify for assistance, like Medicare or food stamps, also qualify for Surge Link’s low-income option.
